# Recording Guide for Ball Mastery Session

This guide will help you record a professional-quality training session video for your client.

---

## Pre-Recording Checklist

### Equipment Needed:
- ✓ Camera/smartphone with good video quality (1080p minimum)
- ✓ Tripod or stable surface for static shots
- ✓ Additional camera/phone for multiple angles (optional)
- ✓ Football (properly inflated)
- ✓ 8-10 cones or markers
- ✓ Measuring tape (for accurate cone placement)
- ✓ Flat, even surface (grass or artificial turf preferred)

### Location Setup:
- ✓ Good lighting (natural daylight is best, avoid harsh shadows)
- ✓ Clear background (minimal distractions)
- ✓ 10x10m minimum space marked out
- ✓ All cones positioned per session plan

### Personal Preparation:
- ✓ Wear appropriate training gear
- ✓ Bring water bottle (stay hydrated between sections)
- ✓ Review session plan thoroughly
- ✓ Practice complex moves before filming if needed

---

## Filming Strategy

### Option 1: Single Continuous Session (Recommended)
**Format:** Record entire 35-minute session in real-time

**Camera Setup:**
- Position camera 5-7 meters away at waist height
- Angle should capture full body and ball
- Ensure entire work area is visible
- Check framing before starting

**Advantages:**
- Shows authentic training flow
- Demonstrates realistic tempo and rest periods
- Client sees complete experience
- Easier to film (press record and go)

**Tips:**
- Start with brief introduction (name, session focus, duration)
- Verbally narrate each section as you transition
- Keep ball in frame at all times
- If you make a mistake, acknowledge it and continue

---

### Option 2: Sectioned with Explanations
**Format:** Film each section separately with demonstrations

**Structure:**
1. Introduction (30 seconds)
2. Section overview + demonstration (repeat for each section)
3. Summary and key takeaways (30 seconds)

**Camera Setup:**
- Main angle: Wide shot showing full body
- Detail shots: Close-up of foot and ball for complex moves
- Alternate between demo speed (slow) and full speed

**Advantages:**
- Clearer instruction for each exercise
- Can show moves from multiple angles
- Easier to edit out mistakes
- More professional tutorial feel

**Tips:**
- Introduce each section: "Now we're moving to Section 2: Foundation Ball Mastery"
- Demonstrate moves slowly first, then at full speed
- Count out loud for timed exercises
- Explain common mistakes and corrections

---

## Filming Guidelines

### General Rules:
1. **Always keep the ball visible** - This is ball mastery, the ball is the star
2. **Maintain good audio** - Speak clearly if narrating
3. **Show full body** - Footwork matters, but body position too
4. **Steady camera** - Use tripod or stable surface
5. **Check footage** - Do a 30-second test recording first

### Verbal Narration Script:

**Introduction:**
```
"Hi, I'm [Your Name], and this is a 35-minute ball mastery training session designed to improve your technical control, close touches, and dribbling skills. This session is suitable for players of all levels from age 8 to professional. We'll work through warm-up, fundamental ball mastery, advanced combinations, game-realistic scenarios, and cool-down. Let's get started."
```

**Section Transitions:**
```
"Section 1: Warm-up - 5 minutes. Our goal is to activate the muscles and introduce the ball..."

"Section 2: Foundation Ball Mastery - 8 minutes. Now we build fundamental control with both feet..."

[Continue for each section]
```

**Closing:**
```
"Great work! This completes our 35-minute ball mastery session. Remember, quality over quantity. Practice these exercises 2-3 times per week, and you'll see significant improvement in your ball control within 4 weeks. Keep working, and I'll see you at the next session."
```

## Camera Angles Guide

### Primary Angle (Main Camera):
- **Distance:** 5-7 meters from subject
- **Height:** Waist to chest height
- **Angle:** Straight on or 45-degree angle
- **Framing:** Full body visible, slight space above head and below feet
- **Purpose:** Shows complete movement and body positioning

### Close-Up Angle (Optional Secondary Camera):
- **Distance:** 2-3 meters from feet
- **Height:** Ground level to knee height
- **Angle:** Focused on feet and ball
- **Framing:** Feet to lower shin visible
- **Purpose:** Shows precise foot placement and ball contact

### Bird's Eye View (Optional for Complex Patterns):
- **Distance:** Directly above work area
- **Height:** 3-4 meters up
- **Angle:** Straight down
- **Framing:** Top-down view of cone patterns
- **Purpose:** Shows spatial awareness and movement patterns

## Video Editing Tips (If Applicable)

### Simple Edits:
- Trim beginning and end to remove setup/breakdown
- Add text overlays for section titles
- Insert timer for each section duration
- Add music during silent portions (keep it low volume)

### Professional Touches:
- Split screen showing real-time + slow-motion
- Text callouts highlighting key coaching points
- Overlay diagrams showing cone setup
- Chapter markers for each section
- Add intro/outro graphics

### Recommended Tools:
- **Free:** iMovie (Mac), DaVinci Resolve, CapCut
- **Paid:** Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro
- **Mobile:** InShot, CapCut, Adobe Premiere Rush

## Common Filming Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Camera too close** - Can't see full body movement
❌ **Poor lighting** - Hard to see ball and footwork
❌ **Shaky footage** - Unprofessional and distracting
❌ **Ball out of frame** - Defeats the purpose
❌ **Speaking too quietly** - Client can't hear instructions
❌ **Too fast** - Client can't follow along
❌ **No introduction/conclusion** - Seems incomplete
❌ **Wrong aspect ratio** - Vertical video for horizontal content

## Post-Recording Steps

1. **Review footage immediately**
   - Watch first 2 minutes to check quality
   - Verify audio/video sync
   - Confirm exercises are visible

2. **Make notes**
   - What worked well
   - What to improve for next recording
   - Any exercises to re-film

3. **Edit if necessary**
   - Trim unnecessary parts
   - Add helpful overlays
   - Adjust audio levels

4. **Export in proper format**
   - Resolution: 1080p (1920x1080)
   - Format: MP4 (H.264 codec)
   - Frame rate: 30fps or 60fps
   - Audio: AAC, 128kbps minimum

5. **Quality check final video**
   - Watch completely before sending
   - Test on different devices
   - Verify file plays correctly

6. **Deliver to client**
   - Include session plan PDF/document
   - Provide any additional notes
   - Request feedback
