# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-05-29

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Pass and move — use the give-and-go (1-2) to break through pressure  
**Context:** team  
**Date:** 2026-05-29  
**Players:** 7–8  
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)  
**Notes:** This session is the natural next step after the receiving work (05-08, 05-15). The players can now take a sharp first touch — so now we ask: *what do you do with it?* The answer is the simplest, most powerful combination in football: the give-and-go (1-2). The one message all session long is **"after you pass, you are not finished — MOVE to receive again."** Every phase repeats that picture: pass, sprint past the defender, get it back, attack the space. Designed for 7 or 8 players — 4v4 is the default; with 7 use 3v3 + 1 floater (always attacks with the team in possession) so no one waits. Evidence base for this design: `docs/training-methodology-evidence.md`.

---

## Equipment Needed

- 8 balls
- 24+ cones or flat markers
- 2 sets of bibs (+ 1 distinct bib for the floater if you have 7 players)
- 4 mini-goals (or 8 cones for 4 cone-goals)

---

## 1. Warm-up — Ball Mastery & Pass-and-Move Activation — 12 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Players wake up their touch, then rehearse the core habit of the whole session — the instant you release the ball, you sprint to a new space. No opponent yet, so the movement is grooved clean.

### Ball Mastery Box → Pass & Follow

*All players in a shared box. First with their own ball (mastery), then we take balls away and the rule becomes: pass, then immediately move to receive again.*

**Duration:** 12 min (3 x ~3.5 min rounds + quick reset)  
**Intensity:** low → moderate  
**Players:** 7–8, all working at once  
**Space:** 15x15m box  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per player (rounds 1–2), then 3–4 balls (round 3)

**Setup:**
Mark a 15x15m box. All players inside.

**Rounds:**
- **Round 1 (ball each):** Coach calls moves — sole rolls, V-drags, inside-inside, toe-taps. Both feet.
- **Round 2 (ball each, variability):** "weak foot only", "head up, call my fingers", "slow… then explode 3m". Never the same rep twice.
- **Round 3 (3–4 balls shared):** Pass-and-move. Whoever has a ball passes to a free teammate, then **sprints to a new space and calls for the next ball**. The picture: ball leaves your foot → you move.

**Coaching Points (Theme: Pass and Move):**
- "The second the ball leaves your foot, GO — don't admire your pass"
- "Pass to feet, then move to a new space — never stand where you passed from"
- "Head up before you pass — find the free player, not just any player"
- "Call for it with your voice AND your run"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* More balls, slower tempo, allow a settle touch before passing
- *Make it harder:* Two-touch maximum (receive, pass), then move
- *Challenge:* One-touch passing in round 3 for the last minute

## 2. Technical — The Give-and-Go (Wall Pass) — 16 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Now we build the actual 1-2. A player passes into a "wall", sprints past the line, and gets it back first-time into space. This is the exact action they will use in the games — grooved here with a cone defender, then with a live one.

### Wall-Pass Lanes

*Pairs work in a lane with a cone acting as the "defender/line". A passes to the wall, runs past the cone, the wall returns it first-time into the space ahead, A drives through a gate.*

**Duration:** 16 min (4 x 3.5 min rounds + rotation)  
**Intensity:** low → moderate  
**Players:** 7–8 in pairs (with 7, make one trio and rotate the wall)  
**Space:** 3–4 lanes of 12x8m  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per lane, cones

**Setup:**
Each lane: a starting cone, a "wall" player standing to the side ~6m up, a single cone (the defender) on the line, and a gate (1.5m) at the far end.

```
 Start ●A
        │  pass
        ▼
       (B) ◄── wall, returns first-time
        │
       [X] ◄── cone = the defender/line
        │  A sprints past X, receives the set
        ▼
       [ G ] ◄── A drives through the gate
```

**Organization for 7–8 players:**
- 4 pairs (8) = continuous work, zero waiting
- 7 players = 3 pairs + 1 trio (in the trio, rotate who is the wall each rep)
- Swap passer / wall every 4–5 reps

**Rounds:**
- **Round 1:** Walk it through slowly. A passes to B, runs past the cone, B sets it back first-time, A drives through the gate.
- **Round 2:** Game speed. The set must be **first-time** and into the space *ahead* of A, not back to A's feet.
- **Round 3:** A disguises it — looks one way, plays the 1-2 the other. (differential learning: vary angle and speed every rep)
- **Round 4:** Replace the cone with a passive live defender (borrow from a neighbouring lane / use the trio's third player). Defender starts slow, A must time the run.

**Coaching Points (Theme: Pass and Move):**
- "Pass firm to the wall, then EXPLODE past the defender — speed is what beats them"
- "Wall player: one touch, set it into the SPACE ahead, not to their feet"
- "Wall player: open your body so you can set it first-time without turning"
- "Timing: don't run before you pass, don't run late — pass and go together"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Wall player can take a settle touch before the return
- *Make it harder:* First-time both ways (A's pass in is one-touch too)
- *Challenge:* The set-back must be played with the weak foot

## 3. 2v1 — Give-and-Go to Beat the Defender — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The 1-2 now has to beat a real, deciding defender. Two attackers, one defender — the perfect picture to force a give-and-go. If you try to dribble through alone you lose it; if you combine, you're through. Compete every rep.

### 2v1 to the Line

*Two attackers vs one defender in a grid. Attackers must get the ball over the far line under control — the easiest way is a 1-2 around the defender.*

**Duration:** 15 min (rounds of ~20s + fast rotation)  
**Intensity:** moderate → high  
**Players:** 7–8 in groups of 3–4  
**Space:** grids of 12x10m  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per grid, cones for the lines

**Setup:**
Each grid: a start line for the attackers, a target line ~12m away. One defender starts in the middle.

```
  Start line
  ●A1     ●A2
     \   /
      \ /        12m
      [D]   ← lone defender
   ─────────── target line
  Cross the line under control = point
```

**Organization for 7–8 players (near-zero waiting):**
- **8 players:** 2 grids of 4 → at each grid, 2v1 live + the 4th is the feeder who starts the next rep instantly, then becomes an attacker. Rotate every rep: defender → feeder → attacker.
- **7 players:** 1 grid of 4 + 1 grid of 3. In the grid of 3 it's straight 2v1 with the scorer/loser becoming defender.
- Reps are ~20 seconds — the "wait" is one rep, so everyone is almost always playing.

**Rules:**
- Attackers score by carrying the ball over the target line under control.
- Defender becomes live as soon as the first attacker touches the ball.
- Reward the 1-2: a line crossed *immediately after a give-and-go* is worth 2; a solo dribble-over is worth 1.
- Defender wins the ball or forces it out = defender's point and they stay (or rotate per your choice).

**Coaching Points (Theme: Pass and Move):**
- "Commit the defender first — run AT them so they have to choose, then play the 1-2"
- "Passer: as soon as you pass, sprint into the space BEHIND the defender"
- "Don't pass too early — make the defender step to you first"
- "Wall: set it first-time into the runner's path, not back where it came from"
- "Win every rep — defender, make them earn it; attackers, full speed"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Defender starts passive (jogging) for the first 2 seconds
- *Make it harder:* Defender starts closer / make the grid narrower so timing is tighter
- *Challenge:* The point only counts if a 1-2 was used (solo dribble = no point)

## 4. Game Form — Constraint Game: "1-2 Counts Double" — 20 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The peak competitive phase. The scoring rule rewards exactly the session habit — combine to break the line — so the teams that pass and move win the game.

### 4v4 (or 3v3 + Floater) — Give-and-Go to Score

*Full small-sided game to mini-goals. A normal goal counts 1; a goal scored within the move directly after a give-and-go counts 3.*

**Duration:** 20 min (4 x 4.5 min rounds + 1 min reset between)  
**Intensity:** HIGH  
**Players:** 8 → 4v4 | 7 → 3v3 + 1 floater  
**Space:** 28x22m pitch  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, 4 mini-goals, bibs

**Setup:**
Mark a 28x22m pitch with 2 mini-goals at each end. No fixed goalkeepers — keeps everyone involved and increases touches.

**Player-count setup:**
- **8:** straight 4v4.
- **7:** 3v3 plus a **floater** (distinct bib) who always plays for whichever team has the ball — so it's always a 4v3 overload going forward. Swap the floater out at each round break.

**Rules:**
- Normal game.
- A normal goal = **1 point**.
- A goal scored on the move **directly after a successful give-and-go (1-2)** in the build-up = **3 points**.
- Quick restarts from the conceding team — keep the ball rolling.

```
   [G]            [G]
 ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
 ┃   B1   R1        ┃
 ┃   (F)  B2  R2    ┃  28m   F = floater (7 players)
 ┃   R3   B3        ┃
 ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
   [G]            [G]

 Pass → sprint past → get it back → finish = 3 points
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Pass and Move):**
- "Look for the wall pass when a defender steps to the ball-carrier"
- "Pass and SPRINT — the give-and-go only works if you move at speed"
- "Wall players: stay one touch, set it into space ahead of the runner"
- "After every pass, ask yourself: where do I run NOW?"

**Coach by questions, not lectures (keep talk under 20 seconds):**
- "What did the defender do when you ran at him? Could you have played a 1-2 there?"
- "When you passed, where did you go? Where SHOULD you have gone?"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Any clean 1-2 anywhere on the pitch earns a bonus point (not just before a goal)
- *Make it harder:* Defenders can't be beaten by a solo dribble in the final third — must combine
- *Challenge:* A 1-2 that goes *around* a defender (not just sideways) is worth 3 even without a goal

## 5. Free Game — 16 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The transfer test. Remove the scoring rule and watch whether they still pass-and-move and look for the 1-2 without being told.

### Free 4v4 / 3v3+Floater with Mini-Goals

*Unrestricted game. Coach stays mostly silent and observes.*

**Duration:** 16 min (5a: 10 min free + 5b: 6 min optional constraint)  
**Intensity:** high → moderate  
**Players:** 8 → 4v4 | 7 → 3v3 + floater  
**Space:** 28x22m  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, 4 mini-goals, bibs

**Setup:**
Same pitch and teams as the Game Form.

**Rules:**
- **5a (10 min):** Normal game, no theme rule. Let them solve it.
- **5b (6 min):** Only if energy and focus are still high — add ONE constraint (2-touch maximum, *or* re-introduce the bonus for a 1-2). If players are fading, skip 5b and just let them play.

**Coach Observation Checklist (internal):**
- Do players move immediately after they pass?
- Do they look for the give-and-go when a defender steps in?
- Do the wall players set it first-time into space?
- Are both feet being used?

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Name the learning. They have repeated one combination all session — now they connect it to real matches.

### Ball Mastery Walk + Reflection

*Walk with a ball to lower the heart rate, then gather for 3 quick questions.*

**Duration:** 5 min total  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 7–8  
**Space:** full training area  
**Equipment:** 1 ball each

**Organization:**
- **2 min:** Walking dribble — inside, outside, sole touches, both feet
- **3 min:** Semicircle reflection with balls on the ground

**Reflection Questions (Theme-Specific):**
1. **"After you pass the ball, what should you do straight away?"**
2. **"What is a give-and-go, and when does it work best?"** (when a defender steps to the ball)
3. **"When in a real match will you use a 1-2 to get past someone?"**

**Close:** "Today we trained the give-and-go — the simplest way two players beat one. Pass, move, get it back. The best teams in the world score goals from exactly this. Remember: when you pass, you are not finished — you are just starting your next run."

## Coach Reminders — 7–8 Players

- **8 players:** 4v4 is the default for every game phase — never create a queue.
- **7 players:** 3v3 + 1 floater (distinct bib) who always attacks with the team in possession — keeps a 4v3 overload and no one sits.
- In the 2v1 block, keep reps to ~20s with instant rotation so the "wait" is never more than one rep.
- No fixed goalkeepers in the game phases — keeps everyone involved, more touches.
- No heading at U9. Shinguards on, no jewellery.
- Fix a failing skill by changing a constraint, not by lecturing.
- Keep coaching interventions under ~20 seconds — coach during play.
- The 1-2 only works at speed — relentlessly reward the SPRINT after the pass, not just the pass.
- If focus collapses late (90 min is long for U9), drop Block 5b and just let them play.
