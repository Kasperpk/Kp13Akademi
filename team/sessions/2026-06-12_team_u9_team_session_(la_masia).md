# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-06-12

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Receive ready, release quick — know your next move BEFORE the ball arrives. First touch takes you somewhere; second touch is the pass or the dribble.
**Context:** team
**Date:** 2026-06-12
**Players:** 9–12
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)
**Notes:** Direct follow-up to the coach log (see `TrainingLog.md`, 12/06): last session (06-10) clicked — players started moving to give the carrier two options, and the possession and free games were visibly better. That stays the season-long backdrop. Today trains the next link in the chain: **now that you've moved and you're free — receive and play QUICK.** Scanning before the ball arrives, first touch in the direction of your next action, and releasing the pass before the defender resets. Second focus from the log: dribbling — so the first touch becomes a decision: *play quick* or *take them on*. The support-movement habit from last session (statue rule, two options) is kept alive inside every exercise rather than re-taught. Evidence base: `docs/training-methodology-evidence.md`.

---

## Equipment Needed

- 12 balls
- 30+ cones or flat markers (2–3 colours — grids + gates)
- 2 sets of bibs + 1 distinct floater bib
- 4 mini-goals

## 1. Warm-up — Ball Mastery & "Scan Before It Arrives" — 12 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Wake up the touch and groove the session habit with no opponent: look BEFORE you receive, first touch into the space you've already chosen, ball gone (or dribble) on touch two.

### Ball Mastery → Scanning Pairs

*First everyone with a ball (mastery + dribble moves), then pairs where the receiver must scan while the ball travels.*

**Duration:** 12 min (3 x ~3.5 min rounds + quick reset)
**Intensity:** low → moderate
**Players:** 9–12, all working at once
**Space:** 18x18m box
**Equipment:** 1 ball per player (round 1), 1 ball per pair (rounds 2–3)

**Rounds:**
- **Round 1 (ball each):** Coach calls moves — sole rolls, V-drags, La Croqueta, body feint + burst, outside-foot cut. Both feet, never the same rep twice.
- **Round 2 (pairs, one ball):** Receiver jogs into a new space; **while the ball travels, the passer raises ONE arm out to a side — that arm is the "defender". The receiver's first touch must go to the OPPOSITE side.** A big binary signal that's readable across a busy box (no finger-counting), and you can check it with your eyes instead of listening for calls. One dribble move, pass back. Swap roles constantly.
- **Round 3 (pairs, one ball):** Same, but **two-touch maximum**: touch one takes you into space, touch two is the return pass. Passer follows their pass into a new angle (keeps last session's "ball moves, you move" alive).

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Ready, Release Quick):**
- "Take your picture BEFORE the ball arrives — eyes up while it travels"
- "First touch is a decision, not a stop — take it WHERE you're going next"
- "Receive across your body with the back foot — then you can see everything"
- "Slow receivers get caught; ready receivers are already gone"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Allow three touches; passer raises the arm early and holds it up
- *Make it harder:* Passer raises the arm late (as the ball is halfway) — forces a second scan
- *Challenge:* One-touch return for the final minute — only possible if the body is open before the ball arrives

## 2. Core Picture — 3v1 Rondo: One-Touch Is Gold — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Same picture as last session — and that's deliberate, it worked. The support movement is now assumed (statue rule still applies); what's NEW is the scoring: quick release is rewarded. A one-touch pass only happens if you moved early, opened your body, and knew your next move — the whole session theme in one rule.

### 3v1 Keep-Ball with Quick-Release Scoring

*Three attackers vs one defender. Passes count double when played first time.*

**Duration:** 15 min (rounds of ~60–90s, defender swaps each round)
**Intensity:** moderate
**Players:** grids of 4 → 12 = 3 grids | 11 = 2 grids of 4 + 1 grid of 3 (2v1, target = 5) | 10 = 2 grids of 5 (4v1, tighter) | 9 = grid of 4 + grid of 5
**Space:** 8x8m per grid (4v1: 7x7m)
**Equipment:** 1 ball per grid + spares, cones

**Rules:**
- 8 on the pass count = 1 point for the attackers.
- **A one-touch pass counts as 2 on the count.** A two-touch pass (touch–release) counts as 1. Three or more touches = 0 (ball stays live, but the count doesn't grow).
- Defender wins it or forces it out = 1 point for the defender; the attacker who lost it becomes the defender.
- **Statue rule stays from last session:** off-ball player standing still while the ball travels = count resets.

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Ready, Release Quick):**
- "You can only play one-touch if you were free BEFORE the ball came — your feet earn your touch"
- "Open your body — receive on the back foot so the next pass is already on"
- "If one-touch isn't safe, don't force it — touch away from the defender, THEN release" (quick, not rushed)
- "Carrier still needs two options — that rule never goes away"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* 9x9m; two-touch passes also count 2
- *Make it harder:* 7x7m
- *Challenge:* Three one-touch passes in a row = instant point regardless of count

## 3. Receive → Beat — 1v1 "First Touch Attack" — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The dribbling focus from the log — but the dribble STARTS with the receive. A pass arrives, a defender arrives, and your first touch decides whether the duel is already half-won. Quick receivers attack a flat-footed defender; slow receivers fight a set one.

### 1v1 to Two Gates after a Live Receive

*Attacker receives a pass into the grid; the moment the pass is played, a defender enters from the far end. Beat them through either end gate.*

**Duration:** 15 min (continuous rotation, ~30s per duel)
**Intensity:** moderate → high
**Players:** grids of 3–4 → 12 = 3 grids of 4 | 11 = 2 grids of 4 + 1 of 3 | 10 = 2 grids of 5 (extra server = faster rotation) | 9 = 3 grids of 3
**Space:** 12x10m per grid, two 2m cone gates on the defender's end line
**Equipment:** 1 ball per grid + spares, cones for gates

**Setup:**
Server stands on one end line with the balls. Attacker starts beside the server, checks away then comes to receive inside the grid. Defender waits on the opposite end line between the two gates.

**Rules:**
- Server plays the pass → defender may sprint in the moment the ball is struck.
- **The server stays live the whole rep:** after serving they slide along their end line as a wall player — the attacker may play a one-two off them to beat the defender (the "quick" road instead of the dribble).
- Attacker gets the ball through **either end gate** (dribbling, or receiving the wall return behind the defender) = 1 point.
- **First touch past the defender (taken so positively the defender never sets) = 2 points.**
- Defender wins it = they attack the server's line for their own point — **and the server becomes the defender**, so the duel flips instantly and the server has to stay switched on.
- Rotate: attacker → defender → server.

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Ready, Release Quick):**
- "Check away, then come — arrive with the ball, not before it"
- "See the defender WHILE the ball travels — your first touch goes where they aren't"
- "Attack the gate they leave open; sell the other one with a feint"
- "Big positive first touch when you have space; soft close touch when they're tight"
- "The wall is always there — if the defender gets set, the one-two is your quick road past them"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Defender starts 2 seconds late
- *Make it harder:* Defender starts as the attacker checks away
- *Challenge:* Server is limited to one touch on the wall return — the one-two only works if the attacker's pass is playable

## 4. Game Form — "Quick or Brave" 3v3 / 4v4 — 20 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The peak competitive phase merges both threads from the log. Two roads to glory: a goal from a QUICK release (assist played within two touches) or a BRAVE dribble (beat your player, then score). Players must read which one the moment demands — that's the game understanding we're chasing.

### Small-Sided Game with Two Bonus Goals
*Game to mini-goals. Normal goal = 1. Quick-release goal or beat-your-player goal = 3.*

**Duration:** 20 min (4 x 4.5 min rounds + 1 min reset)
**Intensity:** HIGH
**Players:** two parallel pitches keep everyone playing
**Space:** 28x22m per pitch
**Equipment:** balls, cones, 4 mini-goals (2 per pitch), bibs

**Player-count setups:**
- **12:** two pitches of 3v3 — same rules on both.
- **11:** one pitch 3v3, one pitch 3v3 + all-time attacking floater (distinct bib); swap the floater each round.
- **10:** one pitch 3v3 + floater, one pitch 2v2 (~20x15m, same rules); rotate between pitches each round.
- **9:** one pitch 3v3, plus 3 players running a 1v1 grid from phase 3 — rotate the trio in each round.

**Rules:**
- Normal goal = **1 point**.
- **QUICK:** goal where the assist was released within two touches of the assister receiving = **3 points**.
- **BRAVE:** goal where the scorer clearly beat an opponent with a dribble in the same possession = **3 points**.
- Quick restarts from the conceding team.

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Ready, Release Quick):**
- "Before the ball comes: quick or brave? Decide while it travels"
- "If a teammate is free — quick. If you have space in front of you — brave. Read it"
- "Support players: your movement from last session is what MAKES quick possible — two options, always"
- "Don't force either one — a forced dribble and a forced pass die the same way"

**Coach by questions, not lectures (keep talk under 20 seconds):**
- "That 3-pointer — what did you see before you received?"
- "You had a free teammate and dribbled into two defenders — what was the quicker road?"
- "How did you know to take them on just then?"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Any one-touch pass in the build-up arms the 3-point bonus
- *Make it harder:* QUICK requires a one-touch assist
- *Challenge:* QUICK + BRAVE in the same possession = 5 points

## 5. Free Game — 14 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The transfer test. No bonuses, no rules. Watch: do players still scan while the ball travels? Is the first touch still a decision? Does the ball move faster than it did two weeks ago?

### Free 3v3 / 4v4 with Mini-Goals

*Unrestricted game. Coach stays mostly silent and observes.*

**Duration:** 14 min (5a: 9 min free + 5b: 5 min optional constraint)
**Intensity:** high → moderate
**Players:** same pitch setups as the Game Form (rotate so anyone who sat in phase 4 plays all of phase 5)
**Space:** 28x22m per pitch
**Equipment:** balls, cones, mini-goals, bibs

**Rules:**
- **5a (9 min):** Normal game, no theme rule.
- **5b (5 min):** Only if energy holds — re-arm ONE bonus (QUICK or BRAVE, not both). If players are fading, skip and just let them play.

**Coach Observation Checklist (internal):**
- Are heads up while the ball travels (scanning), or only after the touch?
- Is the first touch directional, or a stop-then-think?
- Is the time between receive and release shorter than it was on 06-10?
- Are players still giving the carrier two options without being told?
- Does anyone choose the dribble at the RIGHT moment (space in front), not just out of habit?

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Name the learning. The whole session was one question: what do you know before the ball arrives?

### Ball Mastery Walk + Reflection

*Walk with a ball to lower the heart rate, then gather for 3 quick questions.*

**Duration:** 5 min total
**Intensity:** low
**Players:** 9–12
**Space:** full training area
**Equipment:** 1 ball each

**Organization:**
- **2 min:** Walking dribble — inside, outside, sole touches, both feet
- **3 min:** Semicircle reflection with balls on the ground

**Reflection Questions (Theme-Specific):**
1. **"What should you already know before the ball reaches you?"** (where my space is, where the defender is, what my next move is — quick or brave)
2. **"What makes a first touch a GOOD first touch?"** (it goes somewhere — into space, away from pressure, toward my next action)
3. **"When do you play quick, and when do you dribble?"** (quick when a teammate is free; brave when the space in front is mine)

**Close:** "Last time we learned to move so our teammate always has two options — and you did it again today without me asking. That's how a team grows. Today we added the next secret: the best players decide BEFORE the ball arrives. Watch Barcelona — the ball never sleeps, because every receiver already knows their next move. Next match: take your picture early, first touch with a purpose, and then — quick or brave."

## Coach Reminders — 9–12 Players

- **Set up all grids before training:** the 3v1 squares (8x8m) convert to the 1v1 grids (12x10m) by moving a few cones — plan the area so only cones move between phases.
- **12 players** is the clean number: 3 rondo grids → 3 duel grids → two 3v3 pitches. **9–11:** use the per-phase tables; the floater (distinct bib) always attacks with the team in possession.
- Nobody waits more than one short rotation — if you see a queue, split the grid.
- **Don't let "quick" become "panicked."** The one-touch bonus rewards preparation, not hot-potato. If passes get sloppy, say: "quick is a choice you EARN with your first movement" and let the rule keep teaching.
- The statue rule from 06-10 stays in the rondo but doesn't need announcing — apply it once and they'll remember.
- In phase 3 the server has three jobs — firm pass to the back foot, live wall for the one-two, emergency defender if the duel flips. Name all three when you explain the exercise so nobody treats serving as a rest.
- In the Game Form, you are the judge of QUICK and BRAVE — call the bonus loudly the instant it happens so everyone learns what it looks like.
- No fixed goalkeepers. No heading at U9. Shinguards on, no jewellery.
- Keep coaching interventions under ~20 seconds — coach during play.
- **Watch for the moment a player scans, receives on the back foot, and the ball is gone in one touch — stop the world and celebrate it.** That's the next layer of the La Masia picture.
