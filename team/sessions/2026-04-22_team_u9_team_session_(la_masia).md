# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-04-22

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Moving as a unit — how your movement off the ball creates space for teammates, and scanning the game to decide when to pass, dribble, shoot, or run  
**Context:** team  
**Date:** 2026-04-22  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)  
**Notes:** This session introduces collective movement — the idea that you are never isolated on the pitch. Your run without the ball can drag a defender and open a passing lane for a teammate. Your pass-and-move creates a new option. Every phase builds this: move WITH purpose, move FOR your teammate, scan BEFORE you act. Key coaching cue: *"Pass — Move — Show!"* (Pass the ball, move to a new space, show yourself for the next pass.)

---

## Equipment Needed

- 10–12 balls (one per player)
- 20+ cones/flat markers
- 2 sets of bibs (+ 1 neutral colour if available)
- 4 mini-goals (or 8 cones for cone-gates)

---

## 1. Warm-up with Ball — 10 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Introduce the concept of connected movement. Players must maintain a triangle shape while moving — they learn that their position depends on their teammates' position. Ball travels between them, and the whole triangle shifts together.

### "Moving Triangle" — Keep Your Shape

*Groups of 3 with one ball. One player has the ball, the other two move to always form a triangle (roughly equal distances). The triangle moves across the space — pass to a teammate, the whole shape shifts. If the triangle breaks (one player too far or too close), stop and reset.*

**Duration:** 10 min (3 rounds of 2.5 min + transitions)  
**Intensity:** Low → moderate  
**Players:** 10–12 in groups of 3 (3–4 groups; if 10 or 11 players, one group of 4 forms a diamond instead)  
**Space:** 30x20m general area, groups share the space  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per group, 6–8 cones to mark the area boundary

**Setup:**
Groups of 3 form a triangle, roughly 6–8m apart from each other. One ball per group. They move freely inside a 30x20m area (shared with all groups). The challenge: keep the triangle shape while passing and moving.

- **Round 1 (2.5 min):** Free movement. Player with the ball dribbles 3–4m in any direction, then passes. The moment the ball moves, the other two players ADJUST their position to maintain the triangle. Walk/jog pace. Get the habit.
- **Round 2 (2.5 min):** Now add direction — all triangles must move from one end of the 30m area to the other (like progressing up the pitch). Pass and move forward together. Jog back and repeat.
- **Round 3 (2.5 min):** Two-touch maximum. Speed increases. If any two players from the same triangle get closer than 4m, the group does 3 jumping jacks and resets. This teaches spacing awareness.

```
    Round 1: Free movement              Round 2: Progress up the pitch

         A                                  A ──→
        / \      6-8m spacing              / \
       /   \                              /   \
      B ─●─ C   ● = ball with C         B ─●─ C ──→
                                         
    Triangle shifts when ball moves.     All 3 move forward together.
    A adjusts, B adjusts.               Pass-move-shift as a unit.

    Round 3: Two-touch, maintain distance

         A                     ✗ TOO CLOSE!
        / \    6-8m               A
       /   \                      B●  ← only 2m apart
      B ─●─ C  ✓ GOOD             C     → 3 jumping jacks!
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "When the ball moves, YOU move — don't wait, SHIFT your position to keep the triangle"
- "Look at your teammates, not just the ball — where are they? Where SHOULD you be?"
- "The triangle is your team shape. In a real game, if one player runs forward, the others adjust. That starts here."
- "Your movement creates the passing option — if you stand still, there's no triangle to pass into"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Walk pace, unlimited touches, triangle can be any shape
- *Make it harder:* Add a "defender" (one player from another group tries to intercept passes from your triangle while their group rests) — now you move as a unit under pressure
- *Challenge:* Two triangles must move together (6 players, 2 balls) maintaining BOTH triangles — like a real team shape

## 2. Rondo — 12 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** In this rondo, you MUST move after you pass. Standing still after passing means you disappear from the game. The rule forces collective re-organization — when one player moves, passing lanes change, and everyone must adjust. This is the foundation of "moving as a unit."

### Pass-and-Move Rondo (4v2 with Rotation)

*4v2 rondo where the passer MUST move to a new position on the grid after passing. No standing still. If you pass and don't move, you become the defender. This creates constant reshuffling — the group must stay connected.*

**Duration:** 12 min (4 rounds of 2.5 min, rotate defenders each round)  
**Intensity:** Moderate → moderate-high  
**Players:** 10–12 split into two grids (5–6 per grid)  
**Space:** 10x10m per grid  
**Equipment:** Ball, 8 cones (4 per grid), bibs

**Setup (per grid):**

**With 10 players (5 per grid):**
- 3v1 inside + 1 resting, rotate every round

**With 12 players (6 per grid):**
- 4v2 inside = 6 per grid

Mark a 10x10m square. Attackers start on the edges/corners. Defenders in the middle.

**Rules:**
- After you pass, you MUST move to a DIFFERENT edge or corner of the grid. You cannot stay where you are.
- If a passer does not move within 2 seconds of passing, they become a defender (immediate swap).
- Attackers: 2-touch maximum.
- Defenders: when you intercept, the passer who lost it swaps in. New defenders start immediately.
- Point system: 8 consecutive passes = 1 point. But only if EVERY passer moved after their pass.

```
       10m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃  A1 → → ┃     A1 passes to A3, then MOVES
  ┃     ↗    ┃     to a new edge (shown by →→)
  ┃  D  D    ┃     
  ┃          ┃     A2 sees A1 moving, adjusts
  ┃  A2   A3 ┃     position to keep passing lanes open.
  ○━━━━━━━━━━○     
                   Pass → Move → Everyone adjusts

  Before pass:           After pass:
  A1          A4         A4         A1 (moved!)
      D  D                   D  D
  A2    ●A3              A2    ●A3
  
  Triangle changes! Everyone MUST re-scan.
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "Pass and MOVE — the moment the ball leaves your foot, your job isn't done. Find a NEW space."
- "When your teammate moves, the shape CHANGES — scan and adjust YOUR position too. You're connected."
- "Don't all run to the same side — spread out. If one person goes left, someone must cover the right. Think as a UNIT."
- "Before you receive, SCAN: where have your teammates moved to? Where is the open passing lane NOW?"
- "This is exactly what happens in a real match — the ball moves, players move, and you must constantly re-read the game."

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Free touches. Passer has 3 seconds to move (not 2).
- *Make it harder:* One-touch passing. Must move BEFORE the ball arrives to the next player.
- *Challenge:* Add a "gate" (two cones, 1.5m apart) in the middle. Passing through the gate = 3 bonus points. Forces scanning for the split pass while moving.

## 3. Positional Play — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Width and depth — players must stretch the pitch by occupying different zones. The bonus zones reward players who move INTO space away from the group. This teaches them that their off-the-ball positioning creates passing options for the player on the ball. If nobody stretches wide, there's nowhere to play.

### Width and Depth Positioning Game (4v4 + Bonus Zones)

*4v4 inside a main grid. 3m bonus zones on all four sides. A player can step into a bonus zone to receive a pass = 1 point. But you must play back inside within 2 touches. This forces players to stretch wide and deep — creating space for teammates inside.*

**Duration:** 15 min (3 rounds of 4 min + 1 min transitions)  
**Intensity:** Moderate-high  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 20x20m main grid + 3m bonus zones on all sides = 26x26m total  
**Equipment:** Ball, cones (12+ to mark grid and bonus zones), bibs

**Setup:**

Mark a 20x20m main grid with cones. Add a 3m bonus zone on each side (marked with flat markers in a different colour if possible). Total area: 26x26m.

**With 10 players:** 4v4 inside + 2 resting (rotate every 4 min).  
**With 12 players:** 4v4 inside + 2 "bonus zone runners" per team (1 per team on sidelines who can only play in the bonus zones, creating width — rotate every 4 min). Or simply 5v5 + 2 resting.

Simpler: 4v4 + 2 resting. Swap every round.

**Rules:**
- Keep possession inside the main grid. Normal 4v4.
- ANY player can step into a bonus zone at any time to make themselves available for a pass.
- Receiving a pass IN the bonus zone = 1 point for your team.
- After receiving in the bonus zone, you MUST play back inside within 2 touches (cannot stay in the zone).
- Maximum 1 player per team in a bonus zone at any time — you can't just camp there.
- First team to 5 bonus-zone passes wins the round.

```
    ┌──────── bonus zone 3m ────────┐
    │ ┌──────────────────────────┐  │
    │ │                          │  │
    │b│   A   B    A   B         │b │  b = side bonus zones (3m)
    │o│      A   B               │o │  A = Team A (4)
    │n│          A   B    B      │n │  B = Team B (4)
    │u│                          │u │
    │s│            ●             │s │
    │ └──────────────────────────┘  │
    └──────── bonus zone 3m ────────┘
               26m total

    A3 steps into bonus zone →
    A1 passes to A3 in bonus zone = 1 point!
    A3 plays back inside in ≤2 touches.
    A3's run stretches the defense → space opens INSIDE for A2.
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "SCAN the bonus zones — when a teammate runs wide, space opens INSIDE. Use it!"
- "Your run INTO the bonus zone isn't just for you — it PULLS a defender with you and creates space for your teammates inside"
- "Don't all stay in the middle. Think: who is wide? Who is deep? Who is central? Spread as a team."
- "Off the ball: look where your teammates ARE, then go where they are NOT. Fill the empty space."
- "When to run into the bonus zone? When the player on the ball is looking up and needs an option. SHOW for the ball at the right moment."

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* 3 touches inside, unlimited in the bonus zone
- *Make it harder:* 2-touch maximum everywhere. Forces pre-scanning and faster decisions.
- *Challenge:* Bonus zone pass only counts if a DIFFERENT teammate receives the return pass inside (third-man involvement). Now three players must coordinate their movement.

## 4. Game Form — 18 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The combination-only scoring rule forces collective movement under real game pressure. You simply CANNOT score alone. You must combine — give-and-go, third-man run, overlap. This means constant off-the-ball movement, scanning for teammates, and timing your runs. The game PUNISHES isolation and REWARDS moving as a unit.

### 5v5 — Goals Only from Combinations

*5v5 game where goals only count if scored after a combination (give-and-go, wall pass, third-man run, or overlap). Solo goals are disallowed. This creates a game where moving for your teammate becomes essential to scoring.*

**Duration:** 18 min (3 x 5-min games + 1.5 min transitions between)  
**Intensity:** HIGH  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 30x25m pitch with mini-goals at each end  
**Equipment:** Cones (pitch + 2 mini-goals), bibs, ball

**Setup:**

Mark a 30x25m pitch. Mini-goals (2 cones, 2m wide) at each end.

**With 10 players:** 5v5, everyone plays.  
**With 12 players:** 5v5 + 2 resting. Rotate resting pair every 5-min game. Or: 6v6 on a slightly larger pitch (35x25m).

**Rules:**
- Normal small-sided game with one constraint: **goals only count if preceded by a combination.**
- Valid combinations:
  - **Give-and-go (wall pass):** A passes to B, A runs, B returns to A, A scores.
  - **Third-man run:** A passes to B, B lays off, C (third player) runs onto it and scores.
  - **Overlap:** A has ball wide, B overlaps past A, A plays B in, B scores (or crosses for a teammate).
- If a player dribbles solo and scores — goal does NOT count. Restart from the other team.
- Coach briefly explains and demos ONE combination before starting. Keep it short — let the game teach.
- No goalkeepers. Restart from the end line after every goal.

```
  ○━━━━━━[==goal==]━━━━━━○
  ┃                       ┃
  ┃    A1 ──●──→ A2       ┃     Give-and-go:
  ┃     ↗                 ┃     A1 passes to A2
  ┃    A1 ←──●── A2       ┃     A1 runs past
  ┃     ↓                 ┃     A2 returns to A1
  ┃    A1 ──●──→ GOAL!    ┃     A1 scores! ✓ VALID
  ┃                       ┃
  ┃    B1 dribble → GOAL  ┃     Solo goal? ✗ INVALID
  ┃                       ┃
  ┃  A   B   A   B   A    ┃     30m
  ┃    B   A   B   A   B  ┃
  ┃                       ┃
  ○━━━━━━[==goal==]━━━━━━○
            25m

  Third-man:               Overlap:
  A1 → A2 → A3 scores     A1 has ball, B overlaps
                            A1 → B → B scores
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "You CANNOT score alone. You need your teammates. So MOVE for them — make the run that creates the chance."
- "After you pass, DON'T WATCH — run! Your movement after the pass is what creates the combination."
- "Scan BEFORE you receive: where is my teammate running? Can I lay it off for them? Can I play the wall pass?"
- "Timing of the run: not too early (offside feeling, standing waiting), not too late (defender recovers). Arrive as the ball arrives."
- "If one combination doesn't work, recycle possession and try again. Patience — move the defenders, then strike together."

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Any 2-player combination counts (just pass-and-score, someone else must touch the ball before the goal)
- *Make it harder:* 3-player combination required to score (all three different players must touch the ball in the final move)
- *Challenge:* After scoring, the scoring team gets a 10-second "power play" — the other team plays with one fewer player. Rewards combinations even more.

## 5. Free Game — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Transfer:** Coach stays QUIET. Watch: do players move after they pass? Do they create space for each other? Do they look up and scan before deciding to pass, dribble, or shoot? Do they spread out or all cluster around the ball? This is the transfer test.

### Free Play 5v5 or 6v6

*Unrestricted small-sided game. No constraints, no coaching. Let them play.*

**Duration:** 15 min  
**Intensity:** High → moderate (natural game intensity)  
**Players:** 10 (5v5) or 12 (6v6)  
**Space:** 30x20m with mini-goals  
**Equipment:** Cones, bibs, ball, 2 mini-goals (4 cones, 2m wide each)

**Setup:**
Mark a 30x20m pitch. Two mini-goals at each end. Split into two even teams. Play. Coach manages safety and fairness only — no tactical instruction, no freeze-frames, no coaching points.

**What to observe (silently):**
- Are players moving after they pass, or standing and watching?
- Do they spread out to create width and depth, or cluster around the ball?
- Are combinations happening naturally (give-and-gos, third-man runs)?
- Do players scan before receiving — looking for teammates before deciding?
- Is there coordinated movement — when one pushes up, does another cover?

## Alternative Game Form Idea: "Occupy the Lines" (Movement Game → Ball Progression)

> **Red Thread Connection:** This game strips football down to its essence: coordinated off-the-ball movement to break lines. Phase 1 is pure movement — no ball — teaching players to scan, time their runs, and move in unison. Phase 2 adds the ball and introduces the decision: receive, drive, or pass forward. Brilliant for teaching collective movement because you literally CANNOT score alone — all three must move correctly.

### Phase 1: "Occupy the Lines" (No Ball)

*3v3 movement game on a grid with 3 horizontal lines (defense, midfield, attack), each with 3 squares. The attacking team scores by getting 1 player into a square on EACH line simultaneously — but the 3 occupied squares cannot be vertically aligned. Defenders try to occupy the same squares first to block them.*

**Duration:** 8 min (Phase 1) + 10 min (Phase 2) = 18 min total  
**Intensity:** HIGH  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 20x15m grid divided into 3 lines, each line has 3 squares (2x2m marked with cones)  
**Equipment:** Cones (18 cones for 9 squares + grid outline), bibs

**Setup:**

Mark a 20x15m grid. Divide it into 3 horizontal lines (each ~5m deep):
- **Line 1 (bottom):** Defensive line
- **Line 2 (middle):** Midfield line
- **Line 3 (top):** Attacking line

On each line, place 3 squares (2x2m, marked with 4 cones each), evenly spaced across the width (~5m apart). That's 9 squares total (3 per line), arranged in a 3x3 grid pattern.

**Teams:** 3 attackers (blue) vs 3 defenders (red). Remaining players wait and swap in every 2 min.

**With 10 players:** 3v3 + 4 resting (rotate every 2 min — fast rounds).  
**With 12 players:** 3v3 + 6 resting. Or run TWO simultaneous 3v3 grids side by side.

**Starting positions:**
- Attackers: 1 starts in the defensive line area, 1 in midfield, 1 in attacking line.
- Defenders: 1 in each line, marking the attacker in their zone.

**Movement rules for attackers:**
- **Defender (Line 1 player):** Can move across all squares in Line 1, and UP into Line 2 squares.
- **Midfielder (Line 2 player):** Can move across ALL 3 lines (full freedom).
- **Attacker (Line 3 player):** Can move across Line 3 and DOWN into Line 2 squares.

**Scoring (Phase 1):**
- Attackers score 1 point when ALL THREE simultaneously occupy a square on a DIFFERENT line (1 in Line 1, 1 in Line 2, 1 in Line 3) — AND the three squares are NOT vertically aligned (not all in column A, or column B, or column C).
- A square is "occupied" if the attacker is standing inside it with BOTH feet BEFORE a defender can get there.
- Defenders score by preventing this for 30 seconds (run a timer).
- Play 30-second attack rounds, then swap attack/defense.

```
    ATTACKING LINE (Line 3)
    ┌──┐      ┌──┐      ┌──┐
    │A1│      │A2│      │A3│     A1-A3 = squares on attacking line
    └──┘      └──┘      └──┘
   ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─
    MIDFIELD LINE (Line 2)
    ┌──┐      ┌──┐      ┌──┐
    │M1│      │M2│      │M3│     M1-M3 = squares on midfield line
    └──┘      └──┘      └──┘
   ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─
    DEFENSIVE LINE (Line 1)
    ┌──┐      ┌──┐      ┌──┐
    │D1│      │D2│      │D3│     D1-D3 = squares on defensive line
    └──┘      └──┘      └──┘

    20m wide, 15m deep (5m per line)
    Each square = 2x2m

    ✓ VALID SCORE:              ✗ INVALID (vertically aligned):
    Player in D1, M2, A3         Player in D2, M2, A2
    (diagonal = different        (all column 2 = same vertical
    columns = GOOD)              line = NOT allowed)

    Attacker roles:
    DEF player:  Line 1 ←→ + up to Line 2
    MID player:  ALL lines (full freedom)
    ATK player:  Line 3 ←→ + down to Line 2
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "SCAN your teammates — where are they? Which squares are they targeting? Don't pick the same column!"
- "Move in UNISON — all three must arrive at the right moment. Too early and the defender reads it. Too late and you miss your teammate."
- "Fake one direction, go another — drag the defender to one square, then explode to the open one"
- "Communicate! A quick look or gesture to your teammate can coordinate the final move"
- "Midfielder: you have the most freedom — USE it. You're the link. Your movement helps BOTH the defender player and the attacker player"

### Phase 2: "Occupy the Lines" (With Ball)

*Same setup, but now add a ball. The ball starts with the player on the defensive line. If they can get into a square FREE (uncontested), they receive a pass from the coach. Then they must progress the ball forward — either by driving it upward if space allows, or passing to a teammate who has found a free square on the next line. If tagged (touched) by a defender while dribbling, they must stop. The next line must then move to get free and receive.*

**Setup additions:**
- Coach has a ball on the sideline. When the defensive-line attacker reaches a free square, coach plays the ball in to them.
- Once the ball is in play, the game becomes live: the receiver can DRIVE the ball forward through the lines as far as they can, until tagged by a defender (defender touches the ball carrier's shoulder).
- If tagged, the ball carrier must stop AND pass to a teammate who is FREE in a square on the same or higher line.
- If the ball reaches a player in a free square on Line 3 (attacking line), = GOAL (2 points).
- If the ball is played through ALL three lines (one touch in each) = 3 points.
- Defenders can intercept passes. If they win the ball, round resets.

```
    Phase 2: Ball progression through the lines

    ┌──┐      ┌──┐      ┌──┐
    │  │      │★A│      │  │     ★ = attacker FREE in A2 square
    └──┘      └──┘      └──┘           receives pass = GOAL! (2pts)
                  ↑
                  ●  ball passed up
                  ↑
    ┌──┐      ┌──┐      ┌──┐
    │  │      │  │      │★M│     ★ = attacker arrived in M3 FREE
    └──┘      └──┘      └──┘           received ball, drives up
                               ↑ drives ball until tagged
    ┌──┐      ┌──┐      ┌──┐
    │★D│      │  │      │  │     ★ = attacker starts here FREE
    └──┘      └──┘      └──┘           receives ball from coach
     ↑
     ● coach plays ball in

    Flow: Coach → D1 (free) → drives/passes → M3 (free) → passes → A2 = GOAL
    Through all 3 lines = 3 points!
```

**Coaching Points (Phase 2):**
- "Same movement, but now there's a REASON — you're getting free to RECEIVE the ball, not just to occupy space"
- "If you're free, SHOW for the ball — make yourself visible. Open your body to receive"
- "Ball carrier: scan upfield — can you drive? Is there space? Or is a teammate free on the next line?"
- "Decision-making: DRIVE if you have space, PASS if a teammate is free ahead, HOLD if no one is open yet"
- "This is exactly how a real team progresses the ball — defender to midfielder to attacker, breaking lines with movement and passing"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier (Phase 1):* Defenders walk only (attackers can jog). Gives more time to scan and coordinate.
- *Make it harder (Phase 1):* Reduce the occupation time to 2 seconds — all 3 must be in squares simultaneously for a clear 2-count. Forces sharper timing.
- *Make it easier (Phase 2):* Ball carrier gets 3 seconds of "freeze" (defenders can't move) after receiving — time to scan.
- *Make it harder (Phase 2):* Ball MUST be passed (no driving) — forces movement from teammates on the next line. Pure combination.
- *Challenge:* Add a 4th attacker and 4th defender. 4th attacker is a "free roamer" who can go anywhere. Defenders must decide who to track.



## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Reflection:** Bring it back to collective movement. What did they notice about playing together?

### Gentle Dribble + Stretching + Reflection

**Duration:** 5 min (2 min gentle dribble, 3 min stretching + reflection)  
**Intensity:** Low

**Setup:**
- 2 min: Each player dribbles at walking pace. Soft touches, breathing slows down.
- 3 min: Seated in a circle. Light static stretching (hamstrings, quads, hip flexors, calves).

**Reflection Questions (tied to theme):**
- "When you passed the ball today, what did you do AFTER the pass? Why does that matter?"
- "Did you ever make a run that created space for a teammate — even if you didn't get the ball yourself? What happened?"
- "When everyone clusters around the ball, what happens to the space on the pitch? What could you do instead?"
- "What's the difference between moving randomly and moving WITH your teammates? What did the triangle exercise teach you?"
- "In the game at the end, did you notice any moments where your team moved together really well? What did it look like?"
