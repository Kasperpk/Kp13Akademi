# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-06-10

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Support movement — when the ball moves, YOU move. Get free at an angle and give the ball-carrier two options.
**Context:** team
**Date:** 2026-06-10
**Players:** 9–12
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)
**Notes:** Direct response to the tournament observations (see `AAFA training .docx`): the ball doesn't flow in possession games, players run randomly or stand and hope, the central pass is forced because the receiver isn't free AND the passer plays it anyway. The previous sessions built the first touch (05-08, 05-15) and "after you pass, move" (05-29). Today completes the picture: **what is your job when a TEAMMATE has the ball?** The one message all session: **"When the ball moves, you move — never hide, give two options."** The La Masia look the players lack — knowing when to move, when to hold, where the open lane is — is trained here in its simplest forms: 3v1 and 4v2 rondos (support angles + patience), then a central-zone game that attacks the forced-central-pass problem head on: through the middle when it's open, around when it's not. Evidence base: `docs/training-methodology-evidence.md`.

---

## Equipment Needed

- 12 balls
- 30+ cones or flat markers (3 colours if possible — grids + central square)
- 2 sets of bibs + 2 distinct floater bibs
- 4 mini-goals

## 1. Warm-up — Ball Mastery & "Get Free" Activation — 12 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Wake up the touch, then groove the session habit with no opponent: you may only receive if you are MOVING into a new space. Standing still = invisible.

### Ball Mastery Box → "Moving Players Only"

*All players in a shared box. First with their own ball (mastery), then balls are shared and the rule becomes: only a moving player can receive.*

**Duration:** 12 min (3 x ~3.5 min rounds + quick reset)
**Intensity:** low → moderate
**Players:** 9–12, all working at once
**Space:** 18x18m box (slightly bigger than usual for the bigger group)
**Equipment:** 1 ball per player (rounds 1–2), then 4–5 balls (round 3)

**Setup:**
Mark an 18x18m box. All players inside.

**Rounds:**
- **Round 1 (ball each):** Coach calls moves — sole rolls, V-drags, inside-inside, toe-taps, La Croqueta. Both feet.
- **Round 2 (ball each, variability):** "weak foot only", "head up, call my fingers", "slow… then explode 3m", "dribble into the biggest empty space you can see". Never the same rep twice.
- **Round 3 (4–5 balls shared):** **Moving players only.** Whoever has a ball must find a teammate — but the pass only counts if the receiver was *moving into space* when it arrived. A standing player is invisible: carriers are told "you cannot see statues."

**Coaching Points (Theme: Support Movement):**
- "If you stand still, you don't exist — move and you appear"
- "Don't run AT the ball-carrier — run to a space where they can see you"
- "Call with your voice AND your run"
- "Receiver: first touch into the space you're running toward, not back where the ball came from"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* More balls, allow a settle touch
- *Make it harder:* Two-touch maximum; receiver must point to the space before receiving
- *Challenge:* Final minute — you may not receive twice from the same teammate in a row (forces scanning for new options)

## 2. Core Picture — 3v1 Rondo: Always Two Open Lanes — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The simplest possible support picture. One defender, three attackers: the carrier should ALWAYS have two open passing lanes — but only if the two off-ball players keep moving as the ball moves. This is exactly what was missing in the 7v3 / 6v2 possession games: the overload only works if the support players adjust.

### 3v1 Keep-Ball in Small Grids

*Three attackers keep the ball from one defender in a small square. The off-ball players must move every time the ball moves so the carrier always has two options.*

**Duration:** 15 min (rounds of ~60–90s, defender swaps each round)
**Intensity:** moderate
**Players:** grids of 4 → 12 = 3 grids | 11 = 2 grids of 4 + 1 grid of 3 (2v1, target = 5 passes) | 10 = 2 grids of 5 (4v1, tighter grid) | 9 = grid of 4 + grid of 5
**Space:** 8x8m per grid (4v1 grids: 7x7m to keep it honest)
**Equipment:** 1 ball per grid + spares at each grid, cones

**Setup:**
Squares of 8x8m side by side. Attackers move freely inside (not locked to lines — this is about finding angles, not standing on cones).

**Rules:**
- 8 passes in a row = 1 point for the attackers.
- Defender wins it or forces it out = 1 point for the defender, and the attacker who lost it becomes the new defender.
- **The key rule:** if the coach sees a support player standing still while the ball travels, the attackers' pass count resets to zero. ("Statues cost you the count.")

**Coaching Points (Theme: Support Movement):**
- "When the ball moves, YOU move — every pass changes where you need to be"
- "Make an L: never stand in a straight line with the ball and your teammate"
- "Never hide behind the defender — step to where the carrier can SEE you"
- "Carrier: if one lane closes, the other must be open — if it isn't, your teammates are sleeping"
- "First touch away from the defender, not back into them" (touch under pressure)

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* 9x9m grid, or defender at walking pace for the first 10 seconds
- *Make it harder:* 7x7m grid, or two-touch maximum
- *Challenge:* A pass that goes PAST the defender (splits them and the line) counts as 3 passes

## 3. Patience + The Split — 4v2 Rondo: Around Until Through Opens — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** This is the tournament problem in miniature. In matches we force the central pass when the lane is closed. Here the rule rewards exactly the right idea: keep the ball moving AROUND the two defenders (plan B), and the moment the lane between them opens, play THROUGH (the split pass = jackpot). Players learn the central pass is a reward for patience, not a default.

### 4v2 with the Split-Pass Jackpot

*Four attackers vs two defenders. Circulating the ball is good; a pass between the two defenders is gold.*

**Duration:** 15 min (rounds of ~90s, defenders swap each round)
**Intensity:** moderate → high
**Players:** grids of 6 → 12 = 2 grids of 6 | 11 = grid of 6 + grid of 5 (4v1, split = pass across the square past the defender) | 10 = grid of 6 + grid of 4 (3v1 from phase 2) | 9 = grid of 5 + grid of 4
**Space:** 12x12m per 4v2 grid
**Equipment:** 1 ball per grid + spares, cones

**Setup:**
12x12m squares. Four attackers spread around the space, two defenders inside. Attackers may move freely to find angles.

**Rules:**
- 6 passes in a row = 1 point.
- **A split pass (through the gap between the two defenders, received by a teammate) = 3 points** — and the crowd goes wild.
- Defenders win it / force it out = 1 point for them; the attacker who lost it + the attacker who passed it to them become the new defenders.
- Same statue rule as phase 2: off-ball players standing still while the ball travels = count resets.

**Coaching Points (Theme: Support Movement):**
- "You can't force the split — move the ball AROUND until the defenders open the door"
- "Off the ball: when the gap opens, someone must be BEHIND it ready to receive — the split needs a runner, not just a passer"
- "If the middle is closed, going around is not failure — it's how you open the middle"
- "Receiver of the split: move late, arrive as the ball arrives" (timing — don't stand in the gap, appear in it)
- "No panic: you have four against two — breathe, the free man exists, find him"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* 14x14m, 8 passes for the bonus instead of split-only focus
- *Make it harder:* Two-touch maximum for attackers
- *Challenge:* After a successful split, the receiver and passer swap a give-and-go for +1 extra (links last session's 1-2)

## 4. Game Form — Constraint Game: "Through the Middle Counts Triple" — 20 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The peak competitive phase, and the exact tournament picture: a marked central square on the pitch. A goal after a pass is received in the square = 3 points — but the square only works if the receiver MOVES to get free in it and the passer waits for the lane. Forced balls into a marked player get eaten by defenders, and the game teaches what no lecture can.

### 3v3 / 4v4 with a Central Square

*Small-sided game to mini-goals with a 6x6m square in the middle of the pitch. Goals after a controlled reception in the square count triple.*

**Duration:** 20 min (4 x 4.5 min rounds + 1 min reset)
**Intensity:** HIGH
**Players:** see the player-count setups below — two parallel pitches keep everyone playing
**Space:** 28x22m per pitch, 6x6m central square marked in a different colour
**Equipment:** balls, cones, 4 mini-goals (2 per pitch), bibs

**Setup:**
Each pitch: 2 mini-goals at each end (or 1 per end if goals are short), 6x6m square marked dead centre. No fixed goalkeepers.

**Player-count setups:**
- **12:** two pitches of 3v3 — both run the same rules.
- **11:** one pitch 3v3, one pitch 3v3 with the 11th player as an all-time attacking floater (distinct bib); swap the floater each round.
- **10:** one pitch 3v3 + 1 floater, one pitch 2v2 (smaller pitch, ~20x15m, same square rule); rotate players between pitches each round.
- **9:** one pitch 3v3, plus 3 players doing a 2v1 split-grid from phase 3 — rotate the trio in each round so everyone plays the game form at least 3 of 4 rounds.

**Rules:**
- Normal goal = **1 point**.
- A goal scored after a teammate **received a pass inside the central square under control** (anywhere in the same possession) = **3 points**.
- Receiving in the square but losing it immediately = nothing — the touch must survive.
- Quick restarts from the conceding team.

```
   [G]                      [G]
 ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
 ┃         ┌──────┐          ┃
 ┃  B1     │ 6x6  │     R1   ┃  28m
 ┃     B2  │ ZONE │  R2      ┃
 ┃  R3     └──────┘     B3   ┃
 ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
   [G]                      [G]

 Receive in the zone, keep it, team scores = 3 points
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Support Movement):**
- "Don't STAND in the square — defenders will eat you. Appear in it when the lane opens"
- "Passer: if the square is closed, go around — around is how you open the middle" (no forcing!)
- "Check away, then come — drag your defender out, then attack the square"
- "Receiver in the square: know before it arrives — first touch away from pressure, out the other side"
- "Off the ball, everyone: when the ball moves, you move — give the carrier two options ALWAYS"

**Coach by questions, not lectures (keep talk under 20 seconds):**
- "Why did that pass into the square get cut out? Was the lane open or did we force it?"
- "How did you get free in the square just then? Do it again."
- "When the middle is closed — what's plan B?"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Defenders may not enter the square unless the ball is in it
- *Make it harder:* The reception in the square must be followed by a pass OUT of it (turn + play) for the bonus to arm
- *Challenge:* Square reception → give-and-go → goal = 5 points (stacks last session's theme)

## 5. Free Game — 14 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The transfer test. Remove the square bonus and watch: do players still move when the ball moves? Does anyone check away and appear in the middle at the right moment? Does the carrier go around when through is closed?

### Free 3v3 / 4v4 with Mini-Goals

*Unrestricted game. Coach stays mostly silent and observes.*

**Duration:** 14 min (5a: 9 min free + 5b: 5 min optional constraint)
**Intensity:** high → moderate
**Players:** same pitch setups as the Game Form (rotate so anyone who sat in phase 4 plays all of phase 5)
**Space:** 28x22m per pitch (leave the central square marked but say nothing about it)
**Equipment:** balls, cones, mini-goals, bibs

**Rules:**
- **5a (9 min):** Normal game, no theme rule. The square stays on the ground — see who still uses it unprompted.
- **5b (5 min):** Only if energy and focus hold — re-arm ONE constraint (square bonus back on, *or* two-touch max). If players are fading, skip 5b and just let them play.

**Coach Observation Checklist (internal):**
- Do off-ball players move when the ball moves, or do they watch?
- Does anyone check away before coming to receive?
- When the central lane is closed, does the carrier go around — or force it?
- Is the first touch under pressure surviving (away from the defender)?
- Is the ball flowing better than in the 7v3 possession games that triggered this session?

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Name the learning. The whole session was one question: what is my job when my teammate has the ball?

### Ball Mastery Walk + Reflection

*Walk with a ball to lower the heart rate, then gather for 3 quick questions.*

**Duration:** 5 min total
**Intensity:** low
**Players:** 9–12
**Space:** full training area
**Equipment:** 1 ball each

**Organization:**
- **2 min:** Walking dribble — inside, outside, sole touches, both feet
- **3 min:** Semicircle reflection with balls on the ground

**Reflection Questions (Theme-Specific):**
1. **"When your teammate has the ball, what is your job?"** (move, get free where they can see me, give them an option)
2. **"What do you do when the defender blocks your passing lane?"** (move to a new angle — appear somewhere else)
3. **"When do we pass into the middle, and what do we do when it's closed?"** (through when the lane is open; around to OPEN it when it's not — around is not failure)

**Close:** "Today we learned the secret of teams like Barcelona: the player with the ball is never alone, because everyone else is moving for them. When the ball moves, you move. If the middle is closed, we go around — and that's exactly how the middle opens. Next match, watch what happens when all of us give the carrier two options, every single time."

## Coach Reminders — 9–12 Players

- **Set up all grids before training:** the 3v1 squares (8x8m) sit inside/beside the 4v2 squares (12x12m) — only cones move between phases, not players' minds.
- **12 players** is the clean number: 3 rondo grids → 2 split grids → two 3v3 pitches. **9–11:** use the per-phase tables; the floater (distinct bib) always attacks with the team in possession.
- Nobody waits more than one short rotation — if you see a queue, split the grid.
- Defender rotations in the rondos: losing the ball makes you the defender — keeps everyone honest and switches roles constantly.
- **The statue rule is the session.** Resetting the pass count when an off-ball player stands still is what makes support movement non-optional — apply it cheerfully but relentlessly.
- Don't coach the forced central pass with words — let the 3-point square rule and the interceptions teach it. Ask questions afterwards.
- No fixed goalkeepers. No heading at U9. Shinguards on, no jewellery.
- Keep coaching interventions under ~20 seconds — coach during play.
- If focus collapses late, drop 5b and just let them play.
- **Watch for the moment a player checks away and then appears in the square — stop the world and celebrate it.** That's the La Masia picture being born.
