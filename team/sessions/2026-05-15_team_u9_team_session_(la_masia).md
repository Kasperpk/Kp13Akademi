# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-05-15

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Receive with a sharp, directional first touch and scan before the ball arrives  
**Context:** team  
**Date:** 2026-05-15  
**Players:** 8  
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)  
**Notes:** Eight players means 4v4 is the perfect default — every phase below keeps all eight working with no queue. The red thread is the most important skill in the KP13 method: the first touch. Before the ball comes, look. When it arrives, take it somewhere with purpose — away from pressure, into space, forward when possible. Each phase repeats that one message in a new game picture. Evidence base for this design: `docs/training-methodology-evidence.md`.

---

## Equipment Needed

- 8 balls
- 24+ cones or flat markers
- 2 sets of bibs
- 4 mini-goals (or 8 cones for 4 cone-goals)

---

## 1. Warm-up — Ball Mastery & Directional Touch — 12 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Players rehearse the receiving action of the whole session — head up, then a clean directional touch — first with no opponent so the movement is grooved.

### Ball Mastery Box + Coach Calls

*Each player has a ball in a shared box. Coach calls moves, then calls a direction so the first touch always travels somewhere.*

**Duration:** 12 min (3 x 3.5 min rounds + quick reset)  
**Intensity:** low → moderate  
**Players:** 8, all working at once  
**Space:** 15x15m box  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per player

**Setup:**
Mark a 15x15m box. All 8 players dribble inside with their own ball.

**Rounds:**
- **Round 1:** Coach calls moves — sole rolls, V-drags, inside-inside, toe-taps. Both feet.
- **Round 2:** Variability (differential learning) — "weak foot only", "head up, call my fingers", "slow… then explode 3m".
- **Round 3:** Coach calls a direction ("left/right/turn"); players push a sharp first-style touch that way and accelerate into space, avoiding others.

**Coaching Points (Theme: Sharp Directional First Touch):**
- "Touch the ball with purpose — it must travel somewhere, never die under your foot"
- "Head up between touches — you cannot see the picture looking down"
- "Use both feet — two-footed players have twice the options"
- "When I call a direction, the FIRST touch goes there — not the second"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Slow the calls, allow a settle touch first
- *Make it harder:* Two calls in a row (move + direction)
- *Challenge:* Players must call out a number on the coach's raised hand before the next touch

## 2. Technical — First Touch in Pairs — 16 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Now a real pass arrives. Players add the scan before the ball and drive the first touch through a gate, so the touch always has a direction and a destination.

### Receive–Scan–Gate

*Pairs work in a lane. Server feeds, receiver scans over the shoulder, takes a directional first touch through a gate, dribbles out, returns.*

**Duration:** 16 min (4 x 3.5 min rounds + rotation)  
**Intensity:** low → moderate  
**Players:** 8 in 4 pairs (all pairs work at once)  
**Space:** 4 lanes of 10x8m  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per pair, 4 cones per lane

**Setup:**
Create 4 lanes, each 10m long. Server stands at one end, receiver in the middle. Two cone gates (1.5m wide) sit 3m behind the receiver, one left, one right.

**Organization for 8 players:**
- 4 pairs = continuous work, zero waiting
- Swap server / receiver every 60–75 seconds

**Rounds:**
- **Round 1:** Server feeds in. Receiver takes a directional first touch through the gate the coach calls, dribbles out, passes back.
- **Round 2:** Receiver checks the shoulder before the pass, then chooses the free gate alone.
- **Round 3:** Server applies light passive pressure by jogging in after the pass.
- **Round 4:** Receiver must say what they saw over the shoulder before the ball arrives.

```
     10m
 S ● --------- R --------- 
              /   \
           [G1]   [G2]
            3m behind R

 Scan -> open body -> first touch through free gate -> accelerate
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Sharp Directional First Touch):**
- "Check the shoulder BEFORE the ball reaches you, not after"
- "Open your hips — show your body to the space, not to the passer"
- "First touch goes through the gate in ONE touch — don't stop it first"
- "After the touch, accelerate. The touch only matters if you attack the space"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Allow a settle touch, then go through the gate on touch two
- *Make it harder:* Server calls late pressure and chases after the first touch
- *Challenge:* Weak-foot first touch only

## 3. 1v1 — First Touch to Beat the Defender — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The first touch now has to beat an opponent. A good touch makes the 1v1 easy; a bad touch makes it impossible. Compete every rep — intensity is a pillar.

### Receive & Attack 1v1 to Gates

*Four simultaneous 1v1 games. Attacker receives a feed, takes a first touch, beats the defender through a gate.*

**Duration:** 15 min (5 x 2.5 min rounds + rotation)  
**Intensity:** moderate → high  
**Players:** 8 in 4 simultaneous 1v1s  
**Space:** 4 grids of 10x8m  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per grid, 4 cones per grid

**Setup:**
Each grid has a feeder line on one short side and 2 cone gates (1.5m wide) on the opposite side. Attacker starts central, defender starts 4m away.

**Organization for 8 players:**
- 4 pairs, 1 attacker + 1 defender each
- The attacker also acts as their own feeder (self-pass start) OR coach feeds randomly
- Swap attacker/defender every 2 reps

**Rules:**
- Attacker receives, takes a directional first touch, then attacks either gate.
- Defender becomes live the moment the attacker's first touch is taken.
- Point for the attacker who dribbles through a gate under control; point for the defender who wins it cleanly.

```
 Feed ●
       \
        A     vs     D
                    /   \
                 [G1]   [G2]

 First touch sets up the duel -> beat D through a gate
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Sharp Directional First Touch):**
- "Your first touch decides the 1v1 — push it into space, not into the defender"
- "Touch across the defender's body to take them out of the duel"
- "Use a body feint before or with the touch — make the defender move first"
- "Win every rep. Defender or attacker — full intensity"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Defender starts 6m away (more time)
- *Make it harder:* Defender starts 2m away (touch must be sharp and protected)
- *Challenge:* Attacker must beat the defender through the FAR gate

## 4. Game Form — Constraint Game: "First Touch to Score" — 20 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The peak competitive phase. The scoring rule rewards exactly the session habit — a controlled first touch from a pass — so good receiving wins games.

### 4v4 — Receive-to-Finish

*Full 4v4 to mini-goals. The goal only counts if the scorer's touch before shooting was a first touch received from a teammate's pass.*

**Duration:** 20 min (4 x 4.5 min rounds + 1 min reset between)  
**Intensity:** HIGH  
**Players:** 8 — 4v4  
**Space:** 28x22m pitch  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, 4 mini-goals, bibs

**Setup:**
Mark a 28x22m pitch with 2 mini-goals at each end. No fixed goalkeepers — this keeps all 8 involved and increases touches.

**Rules:**
- Normal 4v4 game.
- A goal only counts if the scorer's last touch before shooting was a controlled first touch received directly from a teammate's pass (not a solo dribble-in).
- A scrappy or dead first touch that is then re-controlled does NOT count.
- Restart quickly from the conceding team.

```
   [G]            [G]
 ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
 ┃   B1   R1        ┃
 ┃        B2  R2    ┃  28m
 ┃   R3   B3   R4   ┃
 ┃        B4        ┃
 ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
   [G]            [G]

 Pass -> teammate's first touch -> finish = goal
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Sharp Directional First Touch):**
- "Scan before you ask for the ball — know where the goal and the space are"
- "Receive on the half-turn so the first touch sets up the shot"
- "Passer: hit the back foot so your teammate can finish without re-controlling"
- "Move after you pass — become the next first-touch finisher"

**Coach by questions, not lectures (keep talk under 20 seconds):**
- "Where was the space when you received?"
- "Could your first touch have gone forward instead of square?"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Any first touch from a pass counts, even a heavier one
- *Make it harder:* The first-touch finish must be one-touch (touch = shot)
- *Challenge:* A first-touch finish with the weak foot is worth 2

## 5. Free Game — 16 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The transfer test. Remove the rule and watch whether players still scan and take sharp directional touches without being told.

### Free 4v4 with Mini-Goals

*Unrestricted 4v4. Coach stays mostly silent and observes.*

**Duration:** 16 min (5a: 10 min free + 5b: 6 min optional constraint)  
**Intensity:** high → moderate  
**Players:** 8 — 4v4  
**Space:** 28x22m  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, 4 mini-goals, bibs

**Setup:**
Same pitch and teams as the Game Form.

**Rules:**
- **5a (10 min):** Normal game, no theme rule. Let them solve the game.
- **5b (6 min):** Only if energy and focus are still high — add ONE constraint (2-touch maximum, or a bonus goal for a clean first-touch finish). If players are fading, skip 5b entirely and let them keep playing freely.

**Coach Observation Checklist (internal):**
- Do players scan before receiving?
- Is the first touch directional and into space?
- Do they take the first touch forward when the option is on?
- Are both feet being used?

---

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Name the learning. Players have repeated one receiving action all session — now they connect it to real matches.

### Ball Mastery Walk + Reflection

*Players walk with a ball to lower the heart rate, then gather for 3 quick questions.*

**Duration:** 5 min total  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 8  
**Space:** full training area  
**Equipment:** 1 ball each

**Organization:**
- **2 min:** Walking dribble — inside, outside, and sole touches, both feet
- **3 min:** Semicircle reflection with balls on the ground

**Reflection Questions (Theme-Specific):**
1. **"What did you do BEFORE the ball came to you today?"**
2. **"Where should your first touch go — into your feet, or into space? Why?"**
3. **"When in a real match will you use a sharp first touch?"**

**Close:** "Today we trained the most important skill in football: the first touch. Top players look early, then take one touch that already sets up the next action. That is how you play fast and beat pressure."

## Coach Reminders — 8 Players

- 4v4 is the default for every game phase — never create a queue.
- No fixed goalkeepers in the game phases — keeps all 8 involved, more touches.
- No heading at U9. Shinguards on, no jewellery.
- Fix a failing skill by changing a constraint, not by lecturing.
- Keep coaching interventions under ~20 seconds — coach during play.
- If focus collapses late (90 min is long for U9), drop Block 5b and just let them play.
