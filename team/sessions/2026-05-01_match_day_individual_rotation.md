# Match Day — Individual Rotation Training
### U9 — Kp13 Akademi | 2026-05-01

---

## Context

**Format:** 7v7 | 3×20 min periods | 12 players — 9 play each period, **3 rotate out**  
**Individual training window:** ~18 min per rotation (allow 2–3 min for substitution)  
**Philosophy:** Champions don't do different things. They do the fundamentals better.

---

## Setup (Before the Match Starts)

Mark out the three stations in a corner of the field — away from the pitch so they don't distract the game. Each station needs:
- **3 balls** (one per player, already at each station)
- **8–10 flat cones**
- A wall or fence nearby is a bonus for Station C, but optional

Takes 5 minutes to set up.

---

## The Circuit — 3 Stations, 5 Min Each

Each player goes to a different station. After 5 minutes, they rotate (they track this themselves — give them a timer or tell them to count their reps). After all 3 stations, they've done 15 min and rejoin when called.

They rotate: **A → B → C → done**

---

### Station A — Footwork

> *The foundation. Being able to move correctly and fast*

**Setup:** Two-cones they have to jump over and 5 gates like a ladder.

1. Jump over with both legs then do two-steps in each gate with small sprint 4 times
2. Side Jump over then side step 2 legs in each 3 each side
3. Side jump over and side step with step over
4. Jump over and two steps forward one back


### Station B - Footwork advanced and passing

1. Two steps in with side steps mid-way
2. In and out with sprint
3. Two step in each with pass at end takes turns clockwise

### Station C - Ball work and reaction time with ball
4. Reaction drill without ball - then reaction drill with ball, use cone colors to signal move forward, move backwards. 
5. Tree player passing drills




---

## Key Principle

There is no tactical theme here. That's intentional.

These three stations are the craft. Sole rolls, first touch, dribbling with both feet. The players in the match right now have to figure out when to use these skills. The players in the rotation are sharpening the tools. Both are working.

When they come back into the match, they should feel sharper — more connection with the ball, more confidence in their touch. That's the purpose.

---

## Notes After the Match

- Which station did the players engage with most?
- Was 5 minutes too long / too short per station?
- Any player who stood out (positively or negatively) in their individual work?
- Consider: if one player is weak in a specific fundamental, assign them Station A/B/C twice instead of rotating all three.
