# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-04-15

**Context:** team  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes  
**Notes:** 10 players — Third-man combinations to break lines  

---

## Equipment Needed
- ball
- bibs
- cones
- goals

## 1. Warm-up with Ball — 12 minutes

### Tag Game with Ball
*Everyone dribbles a ball. One player is 'it' and must tag another while dribbling. Tagged player becomes 'it'.*

**Duration:** 5 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 4–20  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball, cones  

**Setup:**
Mark a 15x15m area (smaller = harder). Every player has a ball.
Choose one 'it' player (give them a bib to hold up).
'It' must dribble AND tag someone. Tagged = new 'it'.
Variation: 2 taggers for larger groups.

```
○───────────────○
|                |
|  ●  ●  IT→●   |  15m x 15m
|     ●     ●   |
|  ●     ●      |  IT = tagger (holds bib)
|     ●     ●   |  Everyone has a ball
○───────────────○
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Keep ball close while scanning for tagger
- Use body feints to avoid being tagged
- Head up at all times

### Partner Passing on the Move
*Pairs pass one ball while jogging across the area. Focus on weight of pass, communication, and open body shape.*

**Duration:** 7 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 2–20  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball  

**Setup:**
Pairs, one ball per pair. Use the full area (20x15m).
Both jog forward side by side, passing back and forth.
At the end, turn and come back. Vary: two-touch, one-touch,
different distances apart (5m, 10m).

```
A ─── ● ───→ B        Pairs jog forward
↑            ↑        together, passing
|   5-10m    |        back and forth
|  apart     |
A ←── ● ──── B        Turn and come back
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Open body toward partner
- Call for the ball
- Play to partner's front foot

## 2. Rondo — 15 minutes

### Directional Rondo
*Rondo where the team must advance ball from one end to a target player on the other side. Not just keeping the ball — advancing it.*

**Duration:** 15 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 6–10  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs  

**Setup:**
Mark a 15x10m rectangle with cones. Split into 3 zones (5m
each). Place a target player (T) in each end zone. Middle zone
has 4v2 (or 3v2 with fewer players). Attackers must play from
one target to the other through the middle. Target players
stay in their zones. Point scored for each successful end-to-end
pass sequence.

```
○─────── 10m ───────○
|  T1   :     :  T2  |
|       :     :      |
|       : A B :      |  15m
|       :X   Y:      |
|       : C D :      |  T1,T2 = target players
|       :     :      |  A,B,C,D = possession team
○───────:─────:──────○  X,Y = defenders
 zone1   mid    zone2   Direction: T1 → mid → T2
  5m      5m     5m
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Look forward first before going sideways
- Target player must check shoulders and show for ball
- Combine short passes with one penetrating pass
- Possession with purpose

## 3. Positional Play — 18 minutes

### Third Man Combinations
*Groups of 3. Player A passes to B, B lays off for C (the third man) who plays forward. Rotate positions.*

**Duration:** 8 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 3–12  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball, cones  

**Setup:**
3 cones in a line, ~8m apart. A at first, B at middle, C at far.
Ball starts with A.
Sequence: A passes to B → B lays off first-time → C runs onto layoff.
Rotate: A→B, B→C, C→A.
With 12 players: run 4 groups side by side.

```
A ───pass───→ B
               │
               ↓ layoff
               C ───→ runs onto ball → finish/pass

(cone)        (cone)        (cone)
  8m            8m
Rotate: A→B→C→A after each rep
```

**Coaching Points:**
- C times the run — arrives as ball arrives
- B's layoff is first time and into C's path
- A follows the pass and supports
- This is how Barcelona play through midfield

### Positional Game 4v4+2 Neutrals
*4v4 with two neutral players who always play with possession team (6v4 overload). Neutrals on outside. Teaches playing with numerical advantage.*

**Duration:** 10 min  
**Intensity:** high  
**Players:** 10–10  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs, goals  

**Setup:**
Mark 25x20m grid. Mini-goals at each end.
Team A (4, bibs) vs Team B (4, no bibs).
Two neutrals (yellow bibs) on opposite sidelines.
Neutrals always play with whoever has the ball → 6v4.
Neutrals: max 2 touches, cannot score.
Rotate neutrals every 4 minutes.

```
○══════════════════════○   25m wide
|  [g]            [g]  |
|                      |
N   A  A    B  B       N   N = Neutral (on sideline)
|       B  A   B  A   |   A = Team A (4)
|                      |   B = Team B (4)
○══════════════════════○
Neutrals slide along sideline. Possession team = 6v4.
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Use width — neutrals stretch the play
- Patience in possession — find the right moment to penetrate
- Third man running: receive, pass, move into space
- Defending team: press as a unit, compact shape

## 4. Game Form — 20 minutes

### Game with Mandatory Third Man
*Goals only count if they involve a third-man run. Passer → receiver → third player finishes. Forces combination play.*

**Duration:** 20 min  
**Intensity:** high  
**Players:** 6–14  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs, goals  

**Setup:**
Mark 25x20m pitch with mini-goals at each end.
Equal teams (3v3 to 7v7).
Rule: goal only counts if a THIRD player (not the passer or first receiver)
is involved in the finishing move.
Example: A1 passes to A2, A3 makes run, A2 lays off to A3 → A3 scores. Valid!
A1 dribbles and shoots → NOT valid. Explain + demo before starting.

```
○━━━[goal]━━━○
|              |
|  A1──→A2     |   A1 passes to A2
|        │     |   A2 lays off to A3
|        ↓     |   A3 finishes → GOAL!
|       A3→ ⚽ |   (3rd player must be involved)
|              |
○━━━[goal]━━━○
Solo goals don't count. Combine!
```

**Coaching Points:**
- The third man is the one who wasn't in the pass exchange
- Timing of the run: arrive as ball arrives
- This is Barcelona DNA — third-man movement

## 5. Free Game — 15 minutes

### Free Play (Unrestricted)
*Regular small-sided game with no specific rules or constraints. Let them play. Coach stays quiet. The game is the teacher.*

**Duration:** 15 min  
**Intensity:** high  
**Players:** 4–16  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs, goals  

**Setup:**
Mark pitch appropriate to player count (3v3: 20x15m, 5v5: 30x20m).
Goals at each end. Bibs for teams.
No special rules. No constraints. Just play football.
Coach is SILENT. Only intervene for safety.
This is the most important part of training — the game itself.
Use this to observe: are players using what they learned today?

```
╔═══════════════════════════╗
║ [====GOAL====]            ║
║                           ║
║   A  B    A  B   A       ║   Just play.
║     A  B     B    A  B  ║   No rules.
║                           ║   Coach is quiet.
║ [====GOAL====]            ║   The game is the teacher.
╚═══════════════════════════╝
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Stay quiet — let them solve problems
- Only intervene for safety or unfair play
- Observe: are they applying session themes naturally?

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

### Juggling / Freestyle (For Fun)
*Free juggling or freestyle tricks. No pressure, just enjoy the ball. Celebrates the end of a tough session.*

**Duration:** 2 min  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 1+  
**Space:** minimal 3x3  
**Equipment:** ball  

**Setup:**
Each player has a ball. Spread out — lots of space.
"Free time! Juggle, try tricks, have fun."
No instruction. No pressure. Let them experiment.
For U7-U9: challenge them "Can you beat your record?"
Coach can juggle alongside — model enjoyment.
Duration: 1-3 minutes as a wind-down reward.

```
┌────────────────────────┐
│  ●↑    ●↑      ●↑     │   ● = player
│     ●↑      ●↑        │   ↑ = ball in air (juggling)
│  ●↑      ●↑    ●↑     │
│     ●↑         ●↑     │   Free space. No structure.
└────────────────────────┘   Just enjoy the ball!
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Have fun — no pressure
- Try new tricks you wouldn't normally try
- Celebrate your hard work today

### Group/Individual Reflection
*Guided reflection: What was your best moment? What will you work on next? What did you learn about yourself today?*

**Duration:** 3 min  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 1+  
**Space:** minimal 3x3  

**Setup:**
Players sit in a circle (or semi-circle facing coach).
Coach asks 3 questions — go around the circle:
1. "What was your best moment today?"
2. "What is one thing you want to improve?"
3. "What did you learn?"
For young players (U7-U9): keep it to 1 question, max 2 min.
End with: "Great session. Be proud of your effort."

```
Players seated in circle:

    ●   ●   ●
  ●           ●
  ●   COACH   ●     Coach asks reflection questions.
  ●           ●     Go around circle.
    ●   ●   ●       Keep it short for young players.

"What was your best moment today?"
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Encourage honest self-reflection
- Celebrate effort, not just outcomes
- Positive reinforcement always
