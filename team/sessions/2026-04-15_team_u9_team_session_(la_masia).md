# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-04-15

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Receiving on the half-turn — rotate hips, open body shape, take a decisive first touch away from pressure, and explode into space  
**Context:** team  
**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)  
**Notes:** Inspired by receiving fundamentals — the ability to open the body before the ball arrives, take a directional first touch, and burst away from the marker. Every phase builds the same habit: shoulder check → open hips → first touch into space → accelerate. Key coaching cue for the whole session: *"Shoulder — Hips — Touch — Go!"*

---

## Equipment Needed

- 10–12 balls (one per player)
- 20+ cones/flat markers
- 2 sets of bibs (+ 1 neutral colour if available)
- 4 mini-goals (or 8 cones for cone-gates)

---

## 1. Warm-up with Ball — 10 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Introduce the body shape and hip rotation in a low-pressure paired exercise. No defenders — just building the physical habit of opening up before the ball arrives.

### "Open Up and Go" — Partner Receiving Warm-up

*Pairs facing each other. Passer plays to receiver, who opens body on the half-turn, takes first touch at an angle away from an imaginary marker, then accelerates 3–4m with the ball before passing back.*

**Duration:** 10 min (3 rounds of 2.5 min + transitions)  
**Intensity:** Low → moderate  
**Players:** 10–12 in pairs (5–6 pairs)  
**Space:** 15x15m general area, pairs ~8m apart  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per pair, 1 cone per pair (marker behind receiver)

**Setup:**
Pairs face each other 8m apart. Place one cone 2m behind the receiver — this represents the "marker." Receiver must open their body AWAY from the cone when receiving (i.e., the cone is the defender they're turning away from).

- **Round 1 (2.5 min):** A passes to B. B opens hips to the LEFT, takes first touch left at 45°, accelerates 3m, then passes back. Swap roles every 5 reps. Both sides.
- **Round 2 (2.5 min):** Same but B opens to the RIGHT. Emphasize: the shoulder check happens BEFORE the ball arrives, not during.
- **Round 3 (2.5 min):** A calls "LEFT" or "RIGHT" just before the pass — B must react, open hips to the called side, take first touch that direction, and EXPLODE away for 3–4m. This adds decision-making.

```
     Round 1-2 (set direction)         Round 3 (called direction)

     A ──●──→ B    ○ marker           A ──●──→ B    ○ marker
                   ↑                              ↑
              B opens hips             A calls "LEFT!" or "RIGHT!"
              away from ○             B reacts: opens hips that way
              ↙                        ↙ or ↘
         B accelerates               B explodes away 3-4m
         3-4m with ball

     A ──── 8m ──── B ── 2m ── ○
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "Check your shoulder BEFORE the ball arrives — see where the marker (cone) is"
- "Open your hips toward the space you want to go — your body should be side-on, not square"
- "First touch is decisive — push the ball 1–2m into the space you've opened up, not under your feet"
- "After the touch, EXPLODE — 3–4 fast steps to get away from the marker"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* No cone marker, just practice opening left and right freely
- *Make it harder:* Receiver starts with back to passer (like a real game situation), must spin and open
- *Challenge:* Passer plays a slightly bouncing ball — receiver must still control and turn in one touch

## 2. Rondo — 12 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The directional target forces every player to think about body shape. When you receive from the target, you MUST be half-turned. The rondo punishes closed body shape — if you're square to the ball, you can't see the target and can't play forward.

### Directional Rondo to Targets

*Attackers keep possession against defenders inside a rectangular grid with 1 target player at each end. Objective: play the ball from one target to the other. Targets must receive on the half-turn and play back in. Point scored each time ball reaches opposite target.*

**Duration:** 12 min (4 rounds of 2.5 min, rotate defenders + targets)  
**Intensity:** Moderate → moderate-high  
**Players:** 10–12 split into two grids  
**Space:** 8x12m per grid (rectangular — longer to create forward/backward direction)  
**Equipment:** Ball, 8 cones (4 per grid), bibs

**Setup (per grid):**

**With 10 players (5 per grid):**
- 3v1 inside + 1 target at each end = 5 players per grid

**With 12 players (6 per grid):**
- 3v1 inside + 1 target at each end = 5 active + 1 resting, rotate every round
- Or: 4v1 inside + 1 target = 6 per grid

Mark an 8x12m rectangle. Target players stand beyond each short end line. Attackers keep possession against defender(s). Objective: play ball to the far target. Target receives on the half-turn, plays back inside, and swaps with the passer who sent the ball (passer becomes new target at that end).

```
       8m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃    T1     ┃  ← Target (receives on half-turn, beyond end line)
  ┃ ─ ─ ─ ─  ┃  ← end line
  ┃           ┃
  ┃  A  A     ┃  A = attackers (3)
  ┃     X     ┃  X = defender (1)
  ┃  A     ●  ┃  ● = ball
  ┃           ┃  12m
  ┃ ─ ─ ─ ─  ┃
  ┃    T2     ┃  T2 = target at other end
  ○━━━━━━━━━━○

  Play from T1 end → T2 end = 1 point
  Target receives on half-turn, plays back in,
  then swaps with the passer who sent the ball.
```

**Rules:**
- Ball starts inside. Attackers must reach the target with a pass.
- Target receives on the half-turn (body open to the field), plays back inside in max 2 touches.
- After playing to the target, passer and target swap roles.
- Defender swaps every 2.5 min or after 3 interceptions.
- Point scored every time the ball reaches a target.

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "Target: open your body BEFORE the ball arrives — show the passer which foot you want it on"
- "Hips face the field, not the passer — this lets you play back inside quickly"
- "Inside players: BODY SHAPE — don't face the ball, face half-way between ball and target so you can play forward"
- "First touch takes you toward where you want to play next — never stop the ball dead"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Target has 3 touches (more time to organize body)
- *Make it harder:* Inside players go two-touch maximum — forces pre-scanning and open body
- *Challenge:* Target must play a one-touch return — demands body shape is perfect before the ball arrives

## 3. Positional Play — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The central "link" player is constantly receiving with their back to one direction. They must rotate hips, open body, take a directional first touch, and play forward. Every pass through the middle forces the half-turn under real positional pressure.

### Diamond Receive-and-Turn Positional Game

*Players in a diamond positional structure. Ball must travel through the central player, who always has a semi-active defender. Central player must receive on the half-turn, take a directional first touch, and play forward. Rotate the central role.*

**Duration:** 15 min (rotate central receiver every 2 min)  
**Intensity:** Moderate-high  
**Players:** 10–12 (one large diamond of 8 with 2–4 queuing/resting and rotating in)  
**Space:** 20x20m diamond (tip to tip)  
**Equipment:** Ball, 5 cones (4 diamond points + 1 center), bibs

**Setup:**

Mark a diamond 20x20m (tip to tip). Place a cone at each point (North, East, South, West) and one in the center.

**Positions:**
- **North (N):** 2 players queuing here (forward position — where the ball ends up)
- **South (S):** 2 players queuing (build-up position — where the ball starts)
- **East (E):** 1–2 players
- **West (W):** 1–2 players
- **Center (C):** 1 receiver (the focus player — practices the half-turn)
- **Defender (D):** 1 semi-active defender behind the center player

With 10 players: N(2) + S(2) + E(1) + W(1) + C(1) + D(1) + 2 rotating = 10 ✓  
With 12 players: N(2) + S(2) + E(2) + W(2) + C(1) + D(1) = 12 ✓

**How it works:**
1. Ball starts at **South**. S passes into **Center**.
2. **Center** has a defender (D) behind them (between C and N). Center must check shoulder, open hips, receive on the half-turn AWAY from D.
3. Center's first touch takes the ball toward **East** or **West** (away from D).
4. Center plays to **E** or **W**, who plays forward to **North**.
5. **North** collects and dribbles to **South** queue. Everyone rotates one spot forward. New center player steps in.

```
              ○ N (target)
              ↑
         play forward
              ↑
    W ○ ←── C ──→ ○ E
              ↑
           receive
           half-turn
              ↑         D = semi-active defender
         S ──●──→ C     (behind C, between C and N)

              ○
              S (start)

    Flow: S → C (half-turn) → E or W → N
    Then rotate: N→S queue, everyone shifts.

    Diamond layout (20m tip to tip):

              ○ N
             / \
            /   \
      W ○  / D C \  ○ E       20m
            \   /
             \ /
              ○ S
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "Center: check your shoulder BEFORE the ball leaves South's foot — see where D is"
- "Open your hips away from the defender — if D is on your right, open left (and vice versa)"
- "First touch pushes the ball toward E or W — it's ONE movement: receive and go"
- "After the touch, ACCELERATE into the pass — don't slow down, explode away from D"
- "Weight of pass from South: firm to the back foot so Center can turn in one movement"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Defender is fully passive (standing still) — Center just practices the movement pattern
- *Make it harder:* Defender becomes semi-active (can intercept if Center's touch is poor)
- *Challenge:* Center must play one-touch to E or W — demands perfect body shape before the ball arrives

## 4. Game Form — 18 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The "receive to score" constraint forces players to check their shoulder, open their body, take a directional first touch, and burst away — exactly the session theme — but now under real game pressure with defenders.

### 4v4 to End Zones — "Receive to Score"

*4v4 on a small pitch. Two 3m-deep end zones at each end. You can only score by RECEIVING a pass inside the end zone (not dribbling in). This forces the scorer to time their run, open their body, take a first touch, and explode into the zone.*

**Duration:** 18 min (3-min games, rotate teams if 10–12 players)  
**Intensity:** HIGH  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 25x18m pitch with 3m end zones at each end  
**Equipment:** Cones (pitch outline + end zones), bibs, ball

**Setup:**

Mark a 25x18m pitch. At each end, mark a 3m-deep end zone with cones (dashed line 3m from end line).

**With 10 players:** 4v4 + 2 resting. Rotate resting pair every 3 min (6 x 3-min games).  
**With 12 players:** 4v4 + 4 resting (two pairs). Winner stays on, losers swap with a resting pair.

**Rules:**
- **Score by receiving a pass inside the opponent's end zone.** You cannot dribble into the zone. A teammate must pass to you while you are in (or running into) the zone.
- The receiver must control the ball inside the zone = 1 goal.
- **Bonus (3 goals):** Receiver takes a first-touch TURN inside the zone (receives facing away from the end line, turns with first touch). This is the half-turn under pressure.
- No goalkeeper. Restart from the end line after a goal.
- If ball goes out, restart with a pass from the sideline (no dribble-in).

```
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃ ░░░ END ZONE A ░░░ ┃  3m deep
  ┃ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ┃  ← zone line
  ┃                     ┃
  ┃   A   B    A   B    ┃  A = Team A (4)
  ┃      A   B    A     ┃  B = Team B (4)
  ┃   B          B      ┃           25m
  ┃                     ┃
  ┃ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ┃  ← zone line
  ┃ ░░░ END ZONE B ░░░ ┃  3m deep
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
          18m

  Score: receive pass in end zone = 1 pt
  Bonus: receive on half-turn in zone = 3 pts
  No dribbling into the zone!
```

**Coaching Points (tied to theme):**
- "Before you make your run into the zone, CHECK YOUR SHOULDER — where is the defender?"
- "Open your body as you enter the zone — hips facing the field so you can receive and turn"
- "The first touch takes the ball into the zone, not back toward your teammate"
- "EXPLODE into the zone — sharp burst to create separation from your marker"
- "Passer: play the ball to the BACK FOOT of the runner so they can turn"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* End zone is 5m deep (more space to receive)
- *Make it harder:* Only one defender is allowed to follow into the end zone (1v1 receiving duel)
- *Challenge:* After scoring, the scorer must play out from the zone within 3 seconds — quick transition

## 5. Free Game — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Transfer:** Coach stays QUIET. Watch: do players naturally open their body when receiving? Do they check shoulders? Do they take first touches into space instead of stopping the ball? This is the implicit learning test.

### Free Play 5v5 or 6v6

*Unrestricted small-sided game. No constraints, no coaching. Let them play.*

**Duration:** 15 min  
**Intensity:** High → moderate (natural game intensity)  
**Players:** 10 (5v5) or 12 (6v6)  
**Space:** 30x20m with mini-goals  
**Equipment:** Cones, bibs, ball, 2 mini-goals (4 cones, 2m wide each)

**Setup:**
Mark a 30x20m pitch. Two mini-goals (2 cones, 2m wide) at each end. Split into two even teams. Play. Coach manages safety and fairness only — no tactical instruction, no freeze-frames, no coaching points.

**What to observe (silently):**
- Are players checking shoulders before receiving?
- Are bodies opening on the half-turn, or are they receiving square?
- Are first touches going into space, or stopping the ball dead?
- Are players exploding away from markers after receiving, or standing still?

---

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Reflection:** Bring it back to the theme. What did they feel? When did it work?

### Gentle Dribble + Stretching + Reflection

**Duration:** 5 min (2 min gentle dribble, 3 min stretching + reflection)  
**Intensity:** Low

**Setup:**
- 2 min: Each player dribbles at walking pace. Soft touches, breathing slows down.
- 3 min: Seated in a circle. Light static stretching (quads, hamstrings, hip flexors — hip flexors are especially relevant today since they spent the session rotating hips!).

**Reflection Questions (tied to theme):**
- "When you were about to receive the ball today, what did you do with your body BEFORE the ball arrived?"
- "What's the difference between receiving with your body facing the passer vs. body open to the side?"
- "When did you feel like you really burst away from a defender after receiving? What did that feel like?"
- "Show me with your body: what does 'open hips' look like?"
