# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-05-08

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Open body shape to receive side-on and use the first touch to play or dribble forward  
**Context:** team  
**Date:** 2026-05-08  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)  
**Notes:** Today's red thread is simple and visible for U9s: do not receive square and dead. Scan before the pass, open the hips, take the first touch away from pressure, and attack the space in front of you. Every phase repeats the same message in a new game context: see the picture early, receive on the back foot, and go forward when the moment is there.

---

## Equipment Needed

- 10–12 balls
- 24+ cones or flat markers
- 2 sets of bibs + 1 neutral colour if available
- 4 mini-goals (or 8 cones for 4 cone-goals)

---

## 1. Warm-up with Ball — 10 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Players rehearse the exact receiving action for the whole session: check the shoulder, open the body, receive on the back foot, and drive the first touch into space.

### Shoulder Check Turn Circuit

*Groups of 3 work in a small lane. One player receives from one end, checks over the shoulder, opens up, and takes the first touch through the free gate before passing to the next player.*

**Duration:** 10 min (3 x 3 min rounds + quick rotation)  
**Intensity:** low → moderate  
**Players:** 10–12 in 3 groups of 3–4  
**Space:** 3 lanes of 12x8m  
**Equipment:** 1 ball per lane, 8 cones per lane

**Setup:**
Create 3 lanes, each 12m long and 8m wide. At one end stands Server A, at the opposite end stands Server B. In the middle is the receiver. Two small cone gates (each 1.5m wide) sit 3m behind the receiver, one to the left and one to the right.

**Organization for 10–12 players:**
- 3 groups of 3 = continuous work
- If 11 or 12 players, the 4th player in a group waits behind a server and rotates in after every 2 reps
- 60–75 seconds per player before rotating roles

**Rounds:**
- **Round 1:** Server A passes into the receiver. Receiver checks one shoulder before the ball arrives, opens up, and takes the first touch through the left or right gate chosen by the coach.
- **Round 2:** Same pattern, but now the receiver decides the free side alone. Server gives light passive pressure by jogging toward the receiver after the pass.
- **Round 3:** After turning through the gate, receiver accelerates 3–4m, passes to Server B, then follows the pass and becomes the next server.

```
     12m
 A ● ----------- R ----------- ● B
               /   \
            [G1]   [G2]

 A = server
 R = receiver in middle
 G1/G2 = turn gates 3m behind receiver

 Shoulder check -> open body -> back foot touch -> drive through free gate
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Side-On and Go Forward):**
- "Check your shoulder BEFORE the pass travels all the way to you"
- "Show your chest to one side, not straight at the passer — open hips"
- "Use the back foot so your first touch goes FORWARD, not back into pressure"
- "First touch must travel somewhere with purpose — don't kill the ball"
- "After the turn, accelerate. The turn only matters if you attack the space"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Let the receiver stop the ball first, then turn on the second touch
- *Make it harder:* Server can call late pressure and chase the receiver after the first touch
- *Challenge:* Receiver must call what they saw over the shoulder ("left", "right", or server colour) before the ball arrives

## 2. Rondo — 14 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The rondo adds pressure. Now players must keep the same body shape and first touch habits while reading defenders. The point is not just to keep the ball, but to receive in a way that lets the next pass go forward.

### Directional Rondo with End Neutrals

*Keep possession in the middle and connect from one neutral to the opposite neutral. The scoring pass must travel through a player who receives side-on and plays forward quickly.*

**Duration:** 14 min (5 x 2 min rounds + rotation)  
**Intensity:** moderate → moderate-high  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 16x12m grid with 2 end neutral zones (2m deep)  
**Equipment:** ball, 8 cones, bibs

**Setup:**
Mark a 16x12m rectangle. Add a 2m neutral zone on each short end. Place 1 neutral in each end zone. Inside the grid: 5 attackers vs 2 defenders. One or two players rest depending on numbers and rotate every round.

**Organization for 10–12 players:**
- 10 players: 5 attackers + 2 defenders + 2 neutrals + 1 rest
- 11 players: same setup + 2 resting, rotate one in every 60 seconds
- 12 players: 6 attackers + 2 defenders + 2 neutrals + 2 resting, or keep 5 attackers and rotate frequently for quality

**Rules:**
- Play starts from one neutral.
- Attackers score 1 point by connecting the ball to the opposite neutral.
- The connection only counts if one inside player receives side-on and plays forward in 2 touches or fewer.
- Defenders score 1 point by winning the ball and completing 3 passes with each other or a neutral.
- Rotate defenders every round.

```
      N1 zone
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃              ┃
  ┃  A1  D1  A2  ┃
  ┃      A3      ┃   16m
  ┃  A4  D2  A5  ┃
  ┃              ┃
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
      N2 zone

 N1 -> A3 (side-on) -> N2 = point
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Side-On and Go Forward):**
- "Before the pass, know where the far neutral is — that tells you if you can play forward"
- "Receive on the half-turn so your second touch can be the forward pass"
- "If you receive square, the defenders catch you. If you receive side-on, you can escape"
- "Move off the shoulder of the defender to arrive at an angle, not in a straight line"
- "Neutrals: stand where the forward option is visible. Help the turn happen"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Unlimited touches for the first 2 rounds
- *Make it harder:* Inside players max 2 touches; neutrals one-touch only
- *Challenge:* The forward pass to the far neutral is worth 2 points if it splits both defenders

## 3. Positional Play — 18 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Now the half-turn happens in a more realistic positional picture. Players must find a teammate between lines, receive on the angle, and move the attack forward into the next line.

### 4v4+2 Neutrals — Turn Through the Middle

*Directional possession game. Teams build from one end neutral to the other, but the ball must travel through a central player who receives on the half-turn and plays forward.*

**Duration:** 18 min (4 x 4 min rounds + 2 min total coaching/reset)  
**Intensity:** moderate-high  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 24x18m grid with 3m end zones  
**Equipment:** ball, 10 cones, bibs

**Setup:**
Mark a 24x18m rectangle with a 3m end zone on each short end. One neutral lives in each end zone and plays for the team in possession. In the main area: 4 blue vs 4 red. If 11 or 12 players are present, rotate extra players every 2 minutes as either neutrals or inside players.

**Rules:**
- Teams score 1 point by playing from one end neutral to the opposite end neutral.
- The ball must pass through an inside player before reaching the far neutral.
- That inside player must receive side-on and play forward in 2 touches or fewer.
- If defenders win the ball, they attack the opposite direction immediately.
- Restart from an end neutral after the ball leaves the area.

```
      End Neutral
  ┌──────────────────────┐
  │         N            │  3m zone
  ├──────────────────────┤
  │  B1   R1   B2   R2   │
  │      B3  R3          │  18m
  │  R4   B4             │
  ├──────────────────────┤
  │         N            │  3m zone
  └──────────────────────┘
          24m

 Back neutral -> B3 (half-turn) -> front neutral = point
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Side-On and Go Forward):**
- "Arrive between lines on an angle so you can see BOTH neutrals"
- "Check shoulder early, then open up to the far neutral with your first touch"
- "Your first touch decides if the attack goes forward or dies"
- "Passers: play into the front foot only if your teammate is closed; otherwise hit the back foot so they can turn"
- "After you pass forward, move again. You may become the next support angle"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Allow 3 touches for the central receiver
- *Make it harder:* Far neutral must play one-touch back into the game
- *Challenge:* 2 points if the team connects neutral-to-neutral with only one inside receiver touching the ball in the middle

## 4. Game Form — 20 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** This is the peak. The players must now use the same receiving habit in a competitive game. The scoring rule rewards good body shape and first touch direction instead of random square receptions.

### Turn-and-Attack End Zone Game

*Directional game where a team scores by passing into a teammate arriving in the opponent end zone. The receiver must take the first touch side-on and dribble out through either side gate to finish the action.*

**Duration:** 20 min (4 x 4 min rounds + 1 min between rounds)  
**Intensity:** HIGH  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 32x24m pitch with 3m end zones and 2 side exit gates per end zone  
**Equipment:** cones, bibs, ball

**Setup:**
Mark a 32x24m pitch. Add a 3m end zone at each end. On each end zone line, create 2 side exit gates (1.5m wide each), one near the left touchline and one near the right touchline.

**Organization for 10–12 players:**
- 10 players: 5v5
- 11 players: 5v5 + 1 neutral who plays for possession team in the middle third
- 12 players: 6v6 on the same pitch, or extend to 36x26m if needed

**Rules:**
- Score by passing into a teammate who arrives in the opponent end zone.
- The receiver cannot score by just stopping the ball. They must receive side-on and dribble out through either side gate under control.
- Defenders may recover into the end zone after the pass.
- If the receiver's first touch goes backward or dead, the score does not count even if they escape later.
- Restart quickly from the conceding team.

```
        [G]      END ZONE      [G]
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃                            ┃
  ┃   B1   R1   B2   R2        ┃
  ┃        B3   R3             ┃  32m
  ┃   R4   B4   R5   B5        ┃
  ┃                            ┃
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
        [G]      END ZONE      [G]

 Pass into end zone -> receive side-on -> dribble out through gate = goal
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Receive Side-On and Go Forward):**
- "Time your run into the end zone. Arrive on the move so you can turn"
- "Open your body before the ball arrives — if you wait, you are trapped"
- "First touch goes to space, not to your standing foot"
- "Passer: can you play to the back foot so your teammate can escape through the far gate?"
- "Defenders: force them square. Attackers: beat that pressure with your body shape"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Defender cannot enter the end zone until the receiver's first touch
- *Make it harder:* Receiver must escape through the far-side gate only
- *Challenge:* Goal is worth 2 if the pass into the end zone breaks a line and the receiver gets out in one touch

---

## 5. Free Game — 18 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** This is the transfer test. Remove the rule and see whether the players still scan, open up, and try to play forward naturally.

### Free Play with Mini-Goals

*Unrestricted game. Coach stays silent and watches whether the session theme appears without being forced.*

**Duration:** 18 min (2 x 8 min + 2 min water/reset)  
**Intensity:** high → moderate  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** 30x22m  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, 4 mini-goals, bibs

**Setup:**
Regular small-sided pitch with 2 mini-goals at each end if possible, or 1 mini-goal per end if space is tighter. Keep teams the same as in the Game Form.

**Organization for 10–12 players:**
- 10 players: 5v5
- 12 players: 6v6 or 5v5 + 2 rotating every 2 minutes if intensity is too high
- Coach only intervenes for safety, fairness, or quick restarts

**Rules:**
- Normal game.
- No theme rule.
- Let them solve the game.

**Coach Observation Checklist (internal):**
- Do players check shoulders before receiving?
- Are they receiving side-on instead of square?
- Does the first touch take them away from pressure?
- Do they see forward options earlier than at the start of the session?

---

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Make the learning explicit. The players have repeated the same receiving action all session; now they name it and connect it to match situations.

### Ball Mastery Walk + Reflection

*Players walk with a ball, bring the heart rate down, then gather for 3 quick questions.*

**Duration:** 5 min total  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 10–12  
**Space:** full training area  
**Equipment:** 1 ball each

**Organization:**
- **2 min:** Walking dribble with inside, outside, and sole touches
- **3 min:** Semicircle reflection with balls on the ground

**Reflection Questions (Theme-Specific):**
1. **"What did you do before the ball arrived if you wanted to go forward?"**
2. **"Which foot helped you turn forward today — the front foot or the back foot? Why?"**
3. **"When in a real match can you use that same half-turn?"**

**Close:** "Today we trained something top players do all the time: they see the picture early, open their body, and let the first touch take them forward. That is how you escape pressure and attack quickly."
