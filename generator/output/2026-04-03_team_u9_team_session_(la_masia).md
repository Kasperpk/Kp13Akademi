# U9 Team Session (La Masia) — 2026-04-03

**Session Theme (Red Thread):** Finding the free man in positional overloads  
**Context:** team  
**Date:** 2026-04-03  
**Players:** 7  
**Total Duration:** 90 minutes (including transitions)  
**Notes:** With 7 players, odd numbers naturally create overloads — someone is ALWAYS free. The entire session trains one idea: scan, recognize who is unmarked, and play to them quickly.  

---

## Equipment Needed

- 7 balls (one per player)
- 15–20 cones (flat markers)
- 3 sets of bibs (2 team colours + 1 neutral colour)
- 2 mini-goals (or 4 cones for 2 cone-gates)

---

## 1. Warm-up with Ball — 12 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Introduce scanning and finding the open teammate in a low-pressure, playful environment. No defenders yet — just the habit of looking up and choosing the right pass.

### Possession Circle Warm-up

*Players in a circle, 2 balls moving simultaneously. Pass and follow your pass to a new spot. Who is open? Who already has a ball coming?*

**Duration:** 12 min (3 rounds of 3 min + 1 min transitions)  
**Intensity:** low → moderate  
**Players:** 7 in one circle  
**Space:** Circle with ~5m radius (no cones needed)  
**Equipment:** 2 balls (round 1–2), 3 balls (round 3)  

**Setup:**
All 7 players form a circle, roughly arm's length + 2 steps apart (~5m radius). Start with 2 balls on opposite sides of the circle. No cones needed — players ARE the markers.

**Organization for 7 players:**
- Round 1 (3 min): 2 balls. Free touches. Pass to any open teammate and JOG to their spot. Receiver must move to a new gap before the ball arrives.
- Round 2 (3 min): 2 balls. Two-touch maximum. Call the name BEFORE you pass. If two balls arrive at the same player, that player must quickly redirect one — scan!
- Round 3 (3 min): 3 balls in play. Now there's real cognitive overload. Players must constantly scan: who has a ball coming? Who is free? Where is the open gap?

```
        Round 1-2: 2 balls              Round 3: 3 balls (chaos!)

             P1                              P1
           /    \                          / ●  \
         P7  ●    P2                     P7      P2
         |        |                      |    ●   |
         P6   ●   P3                     P6       P3
          \      /                        \  ●  /
           P5──P4                          P5──P4

      ● = ball (2 moving)            ● = ball (3 moving!)
      Pass + jog to receiver's       Must scan: who is free?
      spot. Receiver moves to        Who already has a ball
      an open gap.                   coming? Don't pass to them!
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Finding the Free Man):**
- "Before you pass, look UP — who is free? Who does NOT have a ball coming to them?"
- "Check BOTH shoulders — scan the full circle before you decide"
- "Call the name of the free man BEFORE the ball leaves your foot"
- "If you see two balls heading to the same player, redirect yours to someone else — that's reading the game"

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Start with 1 ball, add the second after 60 seconds
- *Make it harder:* Add a 4th ball in Round 3 — pure chaos scanning
- *Challenge:* One-touch only in Round 3 — forces pre-scanning

## 2. Rondo — 15 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The neutral player IS the free man. In a tight grid under pressure, the possession team must scan and find the neutral quickly. The neutral is always available — but only if you look for them. This teaches the habit: under pressure → find the overload.

### Rondo with Neutral (4v2+1)

*4 attackers vs 2 defenders with 1 neutral who always plays with the possession team, creating a 5v2. The neutral stays on the edges. Find the neutral = find the free man.*

**Duration:** 15 min (5 rounds of 2.5 min, rotate defenders)  
**Intensity:** moderate → moderate-high  
**Players:** 7 = 4 passers (inside) + 2 defenders (inside) + 1 neutral (edge)  
**Space:** 10x10m grid  
**Equipment:** ball, 4 cones (grid corners), 3 sets of bibs  

**Setup:**
Mark a 10x10m square with 4 flat cones. 4 passers (blue bibs) inside the grid. 2 defenders (red bibs) inside the grid. 1 neutral (yellow bib) moves along the OUTSIDE edges of the grid — they can slide along any edge but cannot enter the grid.

**Organization for 7 players:**
- 4 passers + 2 defenders + 1 neutral = 7 ✓
- Rotate: every 2.5 min, 1 defender becomes neutral, neutral becomes passer, 1 passer becomes defender
- Rotation order ensures everyone defends and plays neutral

**Rules:**
- Possession team has 5v2 overload (4 inside + 1 neutral on edge)
- Neutral receives and plays one-touch or two-touch back inside
- If defenders win the ball, they try to keep it for 3 passes = 1 point
- Possession team: 8 consecutive passes = 1 point. Pass to neutral + return = counts as 2 of the 8
- Defender who intercepts swaps with the passer who lost it (immediate transition)

```
           10m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃                ┃
  ┃   P1    P2     ┃       P1-P4 = passers (blue bibs)
  ┃                ┃       D1, D2 = defenders (red bibs)
N ┃     D1   D2    ┃       N = neutral (yellow bib, edge only)
↕ ┃                ┃
  ┃   P3    P4     ┃  10m  N slides along any outside edge.
  ┃                ┃       Find N = find the free man.
  ┃       ●        ┃       5v2 with possession team.
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○

  Ball play: P3 ──●──→ N (edge) ──●──→ P2 (free man inside)
  Defenders shift to P3 → N is free → quick switch to P2
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Finding the Free Man):**
- "When pressed, the neutral is your SAFE option — but look INSIDE first. Is someone free inside? That's the better pass."
- "Scan BEFORE you receive: where are the defenders? Where is the neutral? Who is the free man AWAY from pressure?"
- "Body shape OPEN — receive sideways so you can see the neutral AND the inside players. Don't receive facing the passer."
- "Speed of play: when you find the free man, play QUICKLY. One extra touch and the free man is no longer free."
- "Defenders: work as a pair. If one presses the ball, the other covers the neutral. Make it hard to find the free man."

**Progressions (apply after 2 rounds):**
- *Round 3:* Two-touch maximum for passers (forces faster scanning)
- *Round 4:* Add a second neutral on the opposite edge — now there are TWO free men to find (6v2 temporarily, but even more scanning required)
- *Round 5:* One-touch for the neutral only — the free man plays at maximum speed

## 3. Positional Play — 18 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** From the rondo's tight overload, now expand to a game-like positional context. Two exercises: first, a perceptual challenge (who is the hidden free man?), then a full positional game where the neutral creates a 4v3 and must be found to break through.

### Exercise A: Free Man Recognition (8 min)

*Possession game where one attacker is secretly "free" (their defender freezes). The attacking team must scan, find the free man, and pass to them to score a point.*

**Duration:** 8 min (4 rounds of 2 min, new "free man" each round)  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 7 = 3v3 + 1 resting (rotates every 2 min)  
**Space:** 18x18m grid  
**Equipment:** ball, 4 cones (grid corners), bibs  

**Setup:**
Mark an 18x18m grid with cones. Team A (3 players, blue bibs) vs Team B (3 players, red bibs). 1 player rests and subs in every 2 min.

**Organization for 7 players:**
- 3v3 inside the grid + 1 resting (rotates in every 2 min)
- Coach walks to Team A, whispers to one player: "You are free."
- That player's defender (Team B) must freeze — cannot press or intercept
- Team A must figure out WHO is free and pass to them to score
- After a point is scored, coach whispers to a NEW free man
- Switch attacking/defending roles every 2 min

**Rules:**
- Team A keeps possession. Team B defends (minus one frozen defender).
- Team A scores 1 point by passing the ball to the free man (the free man must control the ball).
- Team B doesn't know who the free man is — they must figure out which of their players is frozen.
- Change the free man every 60-90 seconds (whisper quietly).
- After 2 min: swap — Team B attacks, Team A defends.

```
            18m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃                    ┃
  ┃   A1  ──→  B1      ┃    A1-A3 = attacking team
  ┃                    ┃    B1-B3 = defending team
  ┃   A2      B2 ❄     ┃    ❄ = frozen defender (can't move)
  ┃                    ┃    ★ = free man (A2's defender frozen)
  ┃   ★A3     B3      ┃         18m
  ┃                    ┃    Coach whispers: "A3, you're free"
  ┃         ●          ┃    B2 freezes. A team must FIND A3.
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○

  A1 has ball → scans → sees A3 is unmarked → pass to ★ = goal!
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Finding the Free Man):**
- "Scan CONSTANTLY — which teammate has space? Who has no defender pressing them? That's your free man."
- "Don't stare at the ball. Lift your head between every touch and CHECK who is open."
- "Free man: SHOW for the ball! Move into space, make yourself visible. Being free means nothing if your teammates can't find you."
- "When you find the free man, the pass must be DECISIVE — firm, on the ground, to their front foot. Don't hesitate or the moment is gone."

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Free man wears a subtle marker (e.g., holds bib in hand) initially
- *Make it harder:* Coach changes the free man every 30 seconds instead of 60–90. Players must scan even faster.

---

### Exercise B: Positional Game 3v3+1 (10 min)

*3v3 with one neutral who always joins the team in possession → 4v3 overload. Score in mini-goals. The neutral IS the free man from the rondo, now in a directional game with goals.*

**Duration:** 10 min (2 x 4 min halves, 2 min break to swap sides/neutral)  
**Intensity:** high  
**Players:** 7 = 3 blue + 3 red + 1 neutral (yellow)  
**Space:** 20x15m grid with mini-goals at each end  
**Equipment:** ball, 4 corner cones, 4 mini-goal cones (2 gates, each 2m wide), bibs  

**Setup:**
Mark a 20x15m grid. Place a mini-goal (2 cones, 2m apart) centered at each short end. Team A (3, blue) attacks one goal, Team B (3, red) attacks the other. Neutral (yellow bib) always joins the team that has the ball → 4v3 in possession at all times.

**Organization for 7 players:**
- 3v3+1 = 7 ✓
- Neutral rotates: swap the neutral player at halftime
- On turnover: neutral instantly switches to the new possession team
- Restart from GK (one player retrieves the ball) after each goal

**Rules:**
- Score by passing or dribbling through the opponent's mini-goal.
- Neutral transitions INSTANTLY on turnover — no pause.
- The possession team always has 4v3. If they can't find the neutral, they waste the overload.
- 3-goal lead rule: if one team is up by 3, quick reset with a new neutral.

```
           20m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃    [==2m==]          ┃    [==] = mini-goal
  ┃                      ┃
  ┃  A1     B1    A2     ┃    A1-A3 = Team A (blue)
  ┃       N              ┃    B1-B3 = Team B (red)
  ┃    B2     A3    B3   ┃    N = neutral (yellow)
  ┃                      ┃    15m
  ┃    [==2m==]          ┃
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○

  Team A has ball → N joins A → 4v3 overload
  A2 ──●──→ N ──●──→ A3 → score through gate!
  Turnover → N instantly switches to Team B → 4v3 for B.
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Finding the Free Man):**
- "You ALWAYS have an extra player — find them! The neutral is your free man. If you're 4v3 and can't score, you're not scanning."
- "After you pass, MOVE — you might become the new free man. Don't stand still."
- "Neutral: position yourself where you create a passing lane through the defense. Don't hide behind a defender."
- "On turnover — IMMEDIATE transition. The neutral switches fast. The team that finds the neutral first controls the overload."
- "Look for the free man AWAY from the ball — the neutral on the weak side is often the most dangerous option."

**Progressions:**
- *Make it easier:* Neutral gets unlimited touches (safety valve)
- *Make it harder:* Neutral is one-touch only — forces quicker combination play
- *Challenge:* Goal only counts if the neutral is involved in the build-up (constraint forces finding the free man)

## 4. Game Form — 20 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** The peak of the session. Everything from warm-up → rondo → positional play converges here. A full game with gates scattered across the pitch — players must scan for the open gate AND the open teammate on the other side. Finding the free man now includes finding the free SPACE.

### Direction Game with Gates (3v3+1 Neutral)

*Scattered cone gates across the pitch. Score by passing through any gate to a teammate on the other side. Neutral always plays with possession team → 4v3. Must scan for open gates AND the open player.*

**Duration:** 20 min (4 x 4 min rounds, 1 min rest between rounds)  
**Intensity:** HIGH (match intensity)  
**Players:** 7 = 3 blue + 3 red + 1 neutral (yellow)  
**Space:** 25x20m pitch  
**Equipment:** ball, 4 boundary cones, 12 cones for 6 gates (each gate 1.5m wide), bibs  

**Setup:**
Mark a 25x20m pitch with corner cones. Scatter 6 cone gates (2 cones each, 1.5m apart) INSIDE the pitch — not on edges. Space them roughly evenly across the area but slightly random (3 in each half).

**Organization for 7 players:**
- 3v3+1 neutral = 7 ✓
- Rotate neutral every round (4 players get to be neutral across the 4 rounds)
- On turnover: neutral instantly joins the new possession team

**Rules:**
- Score = pass through any cone gate to a teammate who controls the ball on the other side
- Cannot score through the same gate twice in a row
- Neutral always plays with the ball team → 4v3 overload
- First to 3 points wins the round. Reset and rotate neutral.
- If no score in 2 min, coach adds a second ball for 30 seconds (increases chaos and scanning opportunities)

```
            25m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃     ╫           ╫        ┃    ╫ = cone gate (1.5m wide)
  ┃  A1      ╫               ┃
  ┃       B1      A2         ┃    A1-A3 = Team A (blue)
  ┃    ╫        N        ╫   ┃    B1-B3 = Team B (red)
  ┃         B2      B3       ┃    N = neutral (yellow)
  ┃   A3        ╫            ┃    20m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○

  Scoring sequence:
  A1 ──●──→ through ╫ ──→ N controls = POINT!
  
  N scans → sees open gate near A3 → N ──●──→ through ╫ → A3 = POINT!
  
  Turnover! B wins ball → N joins B → 4v3 for B now.
  B2 scans for open gate + open teammate...
```

**Coaching Points (Theme: Finding the Free Man):**
- "SCAN before you receive: where is the nearest open gate? Who is on the other side? That's your free man AND your free space."
- "The gates don't move — but the free man does. You need to see BOTH: the gate + the teammate. That's game intelligence."
- "After you score through a gate, the defense will cover it. Switch to a different gate — find the NEW free man on the other side."
- "Neutral: position yourself near gates that are unguarded. You are the easiest free man to find — make yourself obvious."
- "This is what Xavi did 100 times per game: scan, find the open lane, play the ball through it to the free man."

**Progressions (apply across the 4 rounds):**
- *Round 1:* Free touches, get used to the gates
- *Round 2:* Three-touch maximum — forces quicker scanning
- *Round 3:* Goal through a gate is worth 2 points if the NEUTRAL scores the assist (rewards finding the free man)
- *Round 4:* Two-touch maximum — elite speed of play

## 5. Free Game — 18 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** This is the transfer test. NO constraints, no rules, no coaching. Just 3v3+1 with goals. Watch silently: do they look for the free man naturally? Do they use the neutral? Do they scan before receiving? The game is the teacher.

### Free Play (3v3+1 with Mini-Goals)

*Unrestricted small-sided game. Coach stays SILENT. The game is the ultimate teacher.*

**Duration:** 18 min (continuous, or 2 x 8 min with 2 min water break)  
**Intensity:** high → moderate (natural game rhythm)  
**Players:** 7 = 3v3+1 neutral  
**Space:** 25x18m  
**Equipment:** ball, 4 boundary cones, 4 mini-goal cones, bibs  

**Setup:**
Same pitch as Game Form (remove the scattered gates). Mini-goal at each end (2m wide). 3v3+1 — same format as the positional game. Keep the same teams or reshuffle for freshness.

**Organization for 7 players:**
- 3v3+1 = 7 ✓
- Rotate neutral at halftime (9 min mark)
- Coach only intervenes for safety or unfairness. NO tactical instruction.

**Rules:**
- Regular game. Score in the mini-goal.
- Neutral plays with the team in possession.
- That's it. No other rules. Let them play.

```
           25m
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
  ┃     [===GOAL===]       ┃
  ┃                        ┃
  ┃   A    B    N          ┃    Just play.
  ┃      A   B     A      ┃    No rules.
  ┃         B              ┃    Coach is SILENT.
  ┃                        ┃    18m
  ┃     [===GOAL===]       ┃    Watch: do they find
  ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○    the free man naturally?
```

**Coach Observation Checklist (internal, do not share with players):**
- Are players scanning before they receive?
- Do they find the neutral quickly on turnover?
- Is the body shape open (can they see multiple options)?
- Do they switch play to the free man on the weak side?
- Are they communicating — calling for the ball when they're free?

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

> **Red Thread Connection:** Bring the theme to conscious awareness. Players have been practicing "finding the free man" implicitly for 85 minutes. Now ask them to reflect on it explicitly — this cements the learning.

### Gentle Dribbling Cool Down (2 min)

*Walking pace dribbling. Soft touches. Breathing. Heart rate comes down.*

**Duration:** 2 min  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 7 (each with a ball)  
**Space:** Full training area  
**Equipment:** 7 balls  

**Setup:**
Each player collects a ball. Walk slowly around the full training area. Gentle touches — inside, outside, sole. Coach speaks calmly: "Slow it down. Deep breaths. Nice and easy."

```
  ┌──────────────────────────┐
  │  ● →    ● →     ● →     │    ● = player with ball
  │      ● →    ● →         │    → = walking direction
  │  ● →       ● →          │    Walking pace. Soft touches.
  └──────────────────────────┘    Deep breaths. 2 minutes.
```

### Group Reflection — The Free Man (3 min)

*Sit in a circle. Coach asks 3 questions — all tied to the session theme.*

**Duration:** 3 min  
**Players:** 7 seated in a semicircle  
**Equipment:** none  

**Setup:**
Players sit in a semicircle facing the coach. Balls behind them.

**Reflection Questions (Theme-Specific):**
1. **"When did you find the free man today? Can you describe a moment?"** — Let 2–3 players answer. Celebrate specific examples.
2. **"What helped you SEE who was free? What did you do with your eyes or your body?"** — Guide toward: scanning, checking shoulders, open body shape.
3. **"In a real match, who is the 'free man'? Where do you look for them?"** — Connect the session to match day. The answer: the player the defenders have forgotten.

**Close:** "Today we trained one thing that the best players in the world do 100 times per match — finding the free man. Xavi, Iniesta, Pedri — they all look for who is open BEFORE the ball arrives. You did that today. Great effort."
