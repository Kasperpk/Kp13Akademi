## 1. Warm-up with Ball — 12 minutes

### Tag Game with Ball
*Everyone dribbles a ball. One player is 'it' and must tag another while dribbling. Tagged player becomes 'it'.*

**Duration:** 5 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 4–20  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball, cones  

**Setup:**
Mark a 15x15m area (smaller = harder). Every player has a ball.
Choose one 'it' player (give them a bib to hold up).
'It' must dribble AND tag someone. Tagged = new 'it'.
Variation: 2 taggers for larger groups.

```
○───────────────○
|                |
|  ●  ●  IT→●   |  15m x 15m
|     ●     ●   |
|  ●     ●      |  IT = tagger (holds bib)
|     ●     ●   |  Everyone has a ball
○───────────────○
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Keep ball close while scanning for tagger
- Use body feints to avoid being tagged
- Head up at all times

### Partner Passing on the Move
*Pairs pass one ball while jogging across the area. Focus on weight of pass, communication, and open body shape.*

**Duration:** 7 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 2–20  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball  

**Setup:**
Pairs, one ball per pair. Use the full area (20x15m).
Both jog forward side by side, passing back and forth.
At the end, turn and come back. Vary: two-touch, one-touch,
different distances apart (5m, 10m).

```
A ─── ● ───→ B        Pairs jog forward
↑            ↑        together, passing
|   5-10m    |        back and forth
|  apart     |
A ←── ● ──── B        Turn and come back
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Open body toward partner
- Call for the ball
- Play to partner's front foot

## 2. Rondo — 15 minutes

### Rondo with Transition
*Standard rondo but when defenders win ball, they immediately try to score on a mini-goal or dribble out. Attackers must counter-press.*

**Duration:** 8 min  
**Intensity:** high  
**Players:** 5–8  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, goals  

**Setup:**
Mark a 10x10m grid with 4 cones. Place a mini-goal (or cone
gate 2m wide) on one edge outside the grid. 4v2 or 5v2 inside.
On interception, defenders break out and try to score on the
mini-goal. Attackers counter-press immediately. Reset after
goal or ball out. For 8 players: run 5v2 + 1 resting.

```
⊓ ← mini-goal (2m)
10m
○──────────○
|     A     |
|  E        |
D |  X    Y   | B  10m   A,B,C,D,E = passers
|           |          X,Y = defenders
|     C     |          ⊓ = mini-goal (top edge)
○──────────○           On intercept: X/Y → ⊓
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Transition is instant — no pause
- Attackers: counter-press within 3 seconds
- Defenders: first thought is forward when you win it
- Game-realistic intensity on the transition

### Directional Rondo
*Rondo where the team must advance ball from one end to a target player on the other side. Not just keeping the ball — advancing it.*

**Duration:** 7 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 6–10  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs  

**Setup:**
Mark a 15x10m rectangle with cones. Split into 3 zones (5m
each). Place a target player (T) in each end zone. Middle zone
has 4v2 (or 3v2 with fewer players). Attackers must play from
one target to the other through the middle. Target players
stay in their zones. Point scored for each successful end-to-end
pass sequence.

```
○─────── 10m ───────○
|  T1   :     :  T2  |
|       :     :      |
|       : A B :      |  15m
|       :X   Y:      |
|       : C D :      |  T1,T2 = target players
|       :     :      |  A,B,C,D = possession team
○───────:─────:──────○  X,Y = defenders
 zone1   mid    zone2   Direction: T1 → mid → T2
  5m      5m     5m
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Look forward first before going sideways
- Target player must check shoulders and show for ball
- Combine short passes with one penetrating pass
- Possession with purpose

## 3. Positional Play — 18 minutes

### Possession Square (Juego de Posición)
*4v2 or 5v2 in a square grid. Four outside players on the edges, two defenders inside. Outside players must keep possession using only the outside channels. Inside players can intercept. When ball reaches opposite side, point scored. This IS the Barcelona positional play exercise — Guardiola's foundation drill.
*

**Duration:** 10 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 6–8  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs  

**Setup:**
Mark a 10x10m square with cones. Place one player on each side of
the square (4 outside). Two defenders start inside.
Outside players can slide along their edge but cannot enter.
Ball starts with any outside player. Defender who intercepts swaps
with the passer. Run 3-minute rounds, rotate defenders.
With 8 players: two per side on opposite edges.

```
○───A1────○     10m wide square
|         |
A4   D D  A2    D = defenders (inside)
|         |     A1-A4 = outside passers
○───A3────○     ○ = cone
Pass around the square. Split through middle = bonus point.
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Body shape: always open to receive sideways and play forward
- Play away from pressure — if pressed, switch
- Use the 'third man' — the pass after the next pass
- Receive across body to play forward

**Variations:**
- **Two-touch maximum:** Forces quicker decisions and pre-scanning
- **Add inside neutral:** One neutral inside who always plays with possession, creating through-ball option
- **Directional:** Must advance ball to far side to score — possession with penetration

### Third Man Combinations
*Groups of 3. Player A passes to B, B lays off for C (the third man) who plays forward. Rotate positions.*

**Duration:** 8 min  
**Intensity:** moderate  
**Players:** 3–12  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball, cones  

**Setup:**
3 cones in a line, ~8m apart. A at first, B at middle, C at far.
Ball starts with A.
Sequence: A passes to B → B lays off first-time → C runs onto layoff.
Rotate: A→B, B→C, C→A.
With 12 players: run 4 groups side by side.

```
A ───pass───→ B
               │
               ↓ layoff
               C ───→ runs onto ball → finish/pass

(cone)        (cone)        (cone)
  8m            8m
Rotate: A→B→C→A after each rep
```

**Coaching Points:**
- C times the run — arrives as ball arrives
- B's layoff is first time and into C's path
- A follows the pass and supports
- This is how Barcelona play through midfield

## 4. Game Form — 22 minutes

### Game with Mandatory Third Man
*Goals only count if they involve a third-man run. Passer → receiver → third player finishes. Forces combination play.*

**Duration:** 12 min  
**Intensity:** high  
**Players:** 6–14  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs, goals  

**Setup:**
Mark 25x20m pitch with mini-goals at each end.
Equal teams (3v3 to 7v7).
Rule: goal only counts if a THIRD player (not the passer or first receiver)
is involved in the finishing move.
Example: A1 passes to A2, A3 makes run, A2 lays off to A3 → A3 scores. Valid!
A1 dribbles and shoots → NOT valid. Explain + demo before starting.

```
○━━━[goal]━━━○
|              |
|  A1──→A2     |   A1 passes to A2
|        │     |   A2 lays off to A3
|        ↓     |   A3 finishes → GOAL!
|       A3→ ⚽ |   (3rd player must be involved)
|              |
○━━━[goal]━━━○
Solo goals don't count. Combine!
```

**Coaching Points:**
- The third man is the one who wasn't in the pass exchange
- Timing of the run: arrive as ball arrives
- This is Barcelona DNA — third-man movement

### Transition Game (Attack↔Defend)
*On turnover, defending team must counter-attack immediately. Attacking team counter-presses within 3 seconds. Intense transitions.*

**Duration:** 10 min  
**Intensity:** maximum  
**Players:** 6–14  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs, goals  

**Setup:**
Mark 25x20m pitch with mini-goals at each end.
Two equal teams (3v3 to 7v7 depending on numbers).
Rule: on turnover, new attacking team has 6 seconds to shoot.
If they don't shoot in 6 seconds, possession goes back.
Previous attacking team must counter-press within 3 seconds.
Coach counts aloud "1, 2, 3..." to enforce timing.
2-minute rounds with rest between — very intense.

```
○━━━━[goal]━━━━○
|                |
|  A→→→ turnover!|    Turnover happens!
|  B←←← counter! |    B has 6 sec to finish
|    ←A presses   |    A counter-presses in 3 sec
|                |
○━━━━[goal]━━━━○
Coach counts: "1... 2... 3..." on every turnover
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Win ball → first thought is forward
- Lose ball → press instantly (3-second rule)
- Transitions win modern football

## 5. Free Game — 18 minutes

### Free Play (Unrestricted)
*Regular small-sided game with no specific rules or constraints. Let them play. Coach stays quiet. The game is the teacher.*

**Duration:** 18 min  
**Intensity:** high  
**Players:** 4–16  
**Space:** medium 20x20  
**Equipment:** ball, cones, bibs, goals  

**Setup:**
Mark pitch appropriate to player count (3v3: 20x15m, 5v5: 30x20m).
Goals at each end. Bibs for teams.
No special rules. No constraints. Just play football.
Coach is SILENT. Only intervene for safety.
This is the most important part of training — the game itself.
Use this to observe: are players using what they learned today?

```
╔═══════════════════════════╗
║ [====GOAL====]            ║
║                           ║
║   A  B    A  B   A       ║   Just play.
║     A  B     B    A  B  ║   No rules.
║                           ║   Coach is quiet.
║ [====GOAL====]            ║   The game is the teacher.
╚═══════════════════════════╝
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Stay quiet — let them solve problems
- Only intervene for safety or unfair play
- Observe: are they applying session themes naturally?

## 6. Cool Down & Reflection — 5 minutes

### Gentle Dribbling Cool Down
*Soft touches, walking pace dribbling. Focus on breathing and recovery while maintaining ball contact.*

**Duration:** 2 min  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 1+  
**Space:** small 10x10  
**Equipment:** ball  

**Setup:**
Each player has a ball. Use the full training area (no grid needed).
Walk slowly around the space, gently touching the ball with every step.
Alternate: inside foot, outside foot, sole taps.
Coach leads by example — walking pace, calm voice.
Duration: 1-2 minutes. Heart rate should visibly drop.

```
┌──────────────────────┐
│  ● →   ● →          │   ● = player with ball
│       ● →    ● →    │   → = walking direction
│  ● →         ● →    │
│      ● →  ● →       │   Gentle dribble, walking pace
└──────────────────────┘   Use full space, no grid needed
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Breathing: slow, deep breaths
- Lightest possible touches
- Let heart rate come down gradually

### Group/Individual Reflection
*Guided reflection: What was your best moment? What will you work on next? What did you learn about yourself today?*

**Duration:** 3 min  
**Intensity:** low  
**Players:** 1+  
**Space:** minimal 3x3  

**Setup:**
Players sit in a circle (or semi-circle facing coach).
Coach asks 3 questions — go around the circle:
1. "What was your best moment today?"
2. "What is one thing you want to improve?"
3. "What did you learn?"
For young players (U7-U9): keep it to 1 question, max 2 min.
End with: "Great session. Be proud of your effort."

```
Players seated in circle:

    ●   ●   ●
  ●           ●
  ●   COACH   ●     Coach asks reflection questions.
  ●           ●     Go around circle.
    ●   ●   ●       Keep it short for young players.

"What was your best moment today?"
```

**Coaching Points:**
- Encourage honest self-reflection
- Celebrate effort, not just outcomes
- Positive reinforcement always
