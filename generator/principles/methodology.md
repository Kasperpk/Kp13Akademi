# Evidence-Based Coaching Methodology Reference

This document defines the methodology tags used in the exercise library.
Each tag connects to established coaching science research.

---

## Methodology Tags

### `constraints_led`
**Constraints-Led Approach (CLA)**

Manipulate task, environment, or performer constraints to guide learning
without explicit instruction. Players discover solutions through interaction
with the practice environment.

- **In practice:** Change grid size, touch limits, scoring rules, number
  of players, or equipment to create new challenges.
- **Evidence:** Davids, Button & Bennett (2008); Renshaw et al. (2010).
- **Key principle:** The environment teaches, not the coach.

### `differential_learning`
**Differential Learning**

Deliberate variation in movement execution — no two repetitions are identical.
The brain self-organizes toward optimal solutions when exposed to variability.

- **In practice:** Vary speed, angle, surface, distance, rhythm on every rep.
  Never repeat the exact same exercise twice in a row.
- **Evidence:** Schöllhorn (2000); Santos et al. (2018).
- **Key principle:** Variability drives adaptation more than repetition.

### `implicit_learning`
**Implicit (Unconscious) Learning**

Learning occurs through experience and play without explicit awareness
of what is being learned. Players develop skills by doing, not by being told.

- **In practice:** Game-based exercises, rondos, small-sided games.
  Minimize verbal instruction. Let them play and discover.
- **Evidence:** Masters (2000); Poolton & Zachry (2007).
- **Key principle:** What you learn without thinking about, you perform without thinking about.

### `explicit_instruction`
**Explicit (Direct) Instruction**

Coach directly teaches technique, provides demonstrations, and gives
specific feedback. Most effective for foundational technique at early ages.

- **In practice:** Demonstrate a skill, practice in isolation, provide
  corrective feedback.
- **Evidence:** Rink (2010); effective for Ages 6-9 foundational technique.
- **Key principle:** Some things need to be taught directly — especially at young ages.

### `game_realistic`
**Game-Based / Realistic Training**

All exercises mirror or directly relate to match situations. Training
context matches competition context as closely as possible.

- **In practice:** Speed, pressure, decision-making, and spatial
  constraints match what players experience in matches.
- **Evidence:** Bunker & Thorpe (1982) Teaching Games for Understanding (TGfU);
  Ecological Dynamics framework (Araújo et al., 2006).
- **Key principle:** Train how you play. Transfer is highest when practice resembles the game.

### `both_feet`
**Bilateral Development**

Systematic development of the non-dominant foot. Research shows that
bilateral proficiency increases tactical options, reduces predictability,
and is most trainable during the developmental window (ages 6-14).

- **In practice:** 50% of reps on weak foot. Some exercises 100% weak foot.
  Track and enforce actively.
- **Evidence:** Carey et al. (2001); Grouios et al. (2009).
- **Key principle:** Two good feet doubles your options.

### `decision_making`
**Decision-Making Under Pressure**

Exercises that require players to read situations, choose actions,
and execute under time pressure. Develops game intelligence.

- **In practice:** Add defenders, time limits, conditional rules,
  multiple options with trade-offs.
- **Evidence:** Williams & Ford (2008); perception-action coupling research.
- **Key principle:** The best players make better decisions faster.

### `periodization_recovery`
**Periodization & Recovery**

Low-intensity exercises designed for active recovery, flexibility
maintenance, and long-term load management.

- **In practice:** Cool-down activities, light ball work, stretching,
  technical exercises at walking pace.
- **Evidence:** Bompa & Haff (2009); Verheijen Football Periodisation.
- **Key principle:** Recovery is training. You improve during rest.

## Nonlinear Pedagogy (Overarching Framework)

All the above approaches sit within **Nonlinear Pedagogy** — the idea that
learning is not a linear process from instruction to mastery. Instead,
learners self-organize through interaction with task, environment, and
their own capabilities.

**Core tenets:**
1. **Repetition without repetition** — practice the same goal with varied means.
2. **Encourage exploration** — don't prescribe a single "correct" technique.
3. **Manipulate constraints** — rather than instructing, design the environment.
4. **Individual pathways** — each learner finds their own optimal solution.

**Evidence:** Chow et al. (2011); Davids et al. (2013).

## Ecological Dynamics

The theoretical framework underpinning constraints-led and nonlinear approaches.
Key concepts:

- **Affordances:** Opportunities for action that the environment presents.
- **Perception-Action Coupling:** Players perceive and act simultaneously (don't separate).
- **Representative Design:** Practice should represent the real game environment.
- **Task simplification vs. Task decomposition:** Simplify the whole task (fewer players,
  smaller space) rather than breaking it into artificial parts (isolated passing drills).

**Evidence:** Araújo et al. (2006); Fajen et al. (2009).
