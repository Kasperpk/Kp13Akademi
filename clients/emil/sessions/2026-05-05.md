# Session — 2026-05-05

**Date:** 2026-05-05  
**Duration:** 75 minutes  
**Location:**  
**Equipment:** 1 ball, 12-16 cones, 2 mini-goals or 1 goal, bib for defender reference  

---

## Session Theme

**Receive side-on, eliminate the pressure with the first touch, and attack before the defender resets.**

Emil is already elite for his age in technical comfort, two-footedness, intensity, and confidence in 1v1 situations. Today is not about more random reps. It is about sharpening the actions that matter at elite academy level: scanning before the ball arrives, receiving on the back foot, creating separation with the first touch, and finishing before the defender can recover.

La Masia influence for today:

- ball from the first minute
- rondo logic in the session, even in an individual format
- body orientation and positional references matter as much as the touch itself
- both feet are demanded throughout
- the environment teaches the decision, not long coaching speeches

## Warm-Up With Ball — 10 min

Everything starts with the ball. The warm-up already teaches the session theme.

**1. Dynamic movement prep with ball — 3 min**
- Set up a 12x12m square
- Emil dribbles freely with small touches
- Every 5-6 seconds give one cue:
  - **Open** -> open body and take the next touch across the body
  - **Push** -> push 3-4m and accelerate onto it
  - **Check** -> sole on ball, scan over both shoulders, re-start

**Coaching points:**
- Scan before the touch, not after it
- Hips open when receiving the next action
- First acceleration must be immediate after the bigger touch

> Make it easier: larger square, more time between cues. Make it harder: only weaker foot for every second action.

**2. Receive and exit gates — 4 min**
- 12x12m square with 4 exit gates, each 1.5m wide
- Coach stands just outside the square and serves into Emil
- Emil checks shoulder, receives side-on, and must exit through any free gate in 2 touches or less
- 8-10 reps alternating service angle and receiving foot

**Coaching points:**
- Back foot receive whenever the picture allows it
- First touch should already take him away from the imagined defender
- Exit with speed, not with extra control touches

**3. Receive, exit, pass back — 3 min**
- Same setup
- After exiting through a gate, Emil must pass firmly back to coach with the next touch or the touch after
- 6 reps right-foot exit, 6 reps left-foot exit

**Coaching points:**
- Do not admire the first touch, connect it to the next action
- Head comes up during the exit touch
- Quality of pass back matters under movement

## Directional Rondo Foundation — 12 min

La Masia sessions live in the rondo. Here we recreate that logic in an individual session: tight space, body orientation, quick decision, no dead touches.

**Setup**
- 8x8m square
- One central receiving zone marked by 4 small cones, 2x2m
- One target gate on each side of the square, each 1m wide
- Coach serves from different sides of the square

**Exercise: scan, receive, play or turn**
- Emil starts in the central box, side-on stance
- Coach serves from one side
- Before Emil's first touch, coach applies one of three pictures:
  - **Static** -> Emil turns out and dribbles through the opposite gate
  - **Close pressure** -> Emil sets back first time or in two touches
  - **Angle pressure** -> Emil escapes away from pressure through the free side gate
- 3 sets of 3 minutes, 45 seconds reset between sets

**Coaching points:**
- Shoulder check before the ball travels
- Receive on the far foot to protect the ball and face the free side
- The first touch should solve the picture, not just control the ball
- If the picture is closed, secure it quickly and reset

> Progression: step into passive live pressure after the pass so Emil feels recovery from behind.  
> Regression: make coach's body language more obvious and allow unlimited touches for 2 reps before returning to 2-touch maximum.

## Positional Receiving And Turn — 14 min

Now the touches are tied to his real positions: winger and offensive midfielder.

**1. Winger receive on the line — 7 min**
- Set up a 15x10m channel
- Emil starts near the touchline cone, 8-10m from coach
- Coach serves from inside position
- Emil must receive on the back foot and either:
  - drive down the line through a gate 6m ahead, or
  - receive inside and attack a central gate if the outside lane is closed by coach position
- 10-12 reps, alternating sides

**Coaching points:**
- First look is inside, second look is into space
- Back foot opens the whole pitch
- First touch must attack the shoulder of the defender, not stay square

**2. Half-space receive and eliminate — 7 min**
- Set up a diamond: coach at base, Emil in half-space node, finishing gate or mini-goal 12-15m ahead
- Coach serves into Emil with passive recovery pressure from behind after the pass
- Emil receives across body, eliminates the line of pressure, and drives into the finishing gate or mini-goal
- 8 reps each side

**Coaching points:**
- The ball should move across the body, not stop under it
- First two steps after receiving must be explosive
- If the defender is recovering on one shoulder, attack the opposite shoulder immediately

## Directed Game Form — 16 min

This is the peak learning block. The environment forces Emil to read, decide, and attack at elite speed.

**Setup**
- 18x12m channel
- Start zone at one end, receiving zone in the middle, 2 finishing targets at the far end: one central goal and one wide gate
- Coach acts as live recovering defender after each service

**Exercise: receive, eliminate, attack**
- Emil starts in the start zone and checks into the receiving zone
- Coach serves as Emil arrives
- Coach then recovers with one of three behaviors:
  - **Recover flat** -> Emil turns and drives forward aggressively
  - **Recover from inside** -> Emil takes touch away and attacks outside lane
  - **Jump aggressively** -> Emil protects, sets, or bounces and spins into the new lane before receiving the next pass
- 6 x 75-second blocks with 45 seconds rest

**Scoring rules**
- 2 points if Emil eliminates pressure with the first touch and finishes within 4 seconds
- 1 point if he finishes after an extra correction touch
- 0 points if the first touch keeps pressure alive

**Coaching points:**
- Decide before the ball arrives
- First touch must remove the defender's line, not just move the ball
- Attack immediately once the lane is open
- Economy matters: one sharp action beats three decorative touches

> Progression: shorten space to 15x10m and make recovery pressure faster.  
> Variation: start one rep with Emil receiving on weaker foot only.

---

## Finishing From Advantage — 18 min

The point is to score from the advantage created by the receive, not to separate the finishing from the football problem.

**1. Half-space receive to finish across goal — 9 min**
- Set up receiving cone in right half-space and left half-space, 14-18m from goal or mini-goal
- Coach serves from behind and inside Emil
- Emil receives on the back foot, drives diagonally inside, and finishes across goal with the far corner as reference
- 8 reps each side, both feet required

**Coaching points:**
- First touch creates the shooting lane
- Strike before the defender resets on the shoulder
- Non-kicking foot sets quickly, no extra stutter steps

**2. Wide receive, separate, finish — 9 min**
- Start wide in a 12x8m wing channel
- Coach serves to feet, then applies passive-to-live pressure from inside shoulder
- Emil uses one efficient touch or body feint to create one yard and finishes near post or far post depending on angle
- 10-12 total reps, alternating sides and finishing foot

**Coaching points:**
- Separation touch must be realistic, not theatrical
- Shoot off the created gap quickly
- Both feet stay alive; weak foot is not a bonus, it is expected

## Cool Down & Reflection — 5 min

Light dribble, easy passing, slower breathing.

Ask:

- When did you know early enough that you could turn?
- Which first touch killed the pressure best today?
- Which finish came from the cleanest advantage, not the best strike?

- Emil starts scanning before the pass more consistently by the middle of the session
- More first touches travel across his body and away from pressure
- Finishes come earlier in the action, with less hesitation
- Right and left foot both appear naturally in receiving and finishing actions
