# Session — 2026-04-14

**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Location:**  
**Equipment:** 1 ball, 12+ cones, 2 mini-goals

## Session Theme

**Driving the ball with speed to break lines — and knowing WHEN to drive vs. pass.**

Felix's passing is already elite for his age. The next level is becoming unpredictable: a central player who can circulate AND drive through space with force. Today we build the driving technique, then force live decisions where the environment tells him drive or pass — not the coach.

---

## Warm-Up & Activation — 8 min

Activate the body and introduce the driving touch pattern from the very first ball contact. No laps without the ball.

**1. Dynamic movement prep (no ball) — 2 min**
- Set up a 10m lane between two cones
- High knees down, heel flicks back — 2x
- Single-leg hops: 5 right, 5 left, focus on SOFT landing and balance
- Broad jumps: 4 two-footed jumps, stick the landing — builds the explosive push-off we need for driving

**2. Ball activation — driving tempo changes — 3 min**
- 15x15m box marked with 4 cones
- Felix dribbles freely inside the box — CLOSE control, small touches
- On "GO": push the ball 3m ahead with laces and SPRINT to it (this is the driving touch)
- On "STOP": sole on ball, freeze, look up, scan the space, go again
- Continuous for 3 min — alternate every 8-10 seconds

```
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
┃    15x15m      ┃
┃                ┃
┃   F ──●──→     ┃  "GO" = push ball ahead, sprint
┃        ↓       ┃
┃       ●̣ STOP   ┃  "STOP" = sole, head up, scan
┃                ┃
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
```

**3. Driving lines with finish — 3 min**
- 2 cones 18m apart, mini-goal at the end
- Felix drives from cone A to cone B at 80% pace: 2-3 touches with laces/outside of foot, each touch pushes the ball 3-4m ahead
- At the end: finish into the mini-goal with the last touch
- 6 reps: 3 right foot driving, 3 left foot driving

```
         18m
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━[GOAL]
F ──●──→  →  →  →  ⚽
  push  run  push  FINISH
```

**Coaching points:**
- The driving touch is NOT a dribbling touch — push firmly ahead with laces or outside, then sprint onto it
- Head comes up between touches — "in a game you're driving TOWARD something, you need to see it"
- Arms pump naturally like sprinting — the ball doesn't slow you down

> Make it easier: shorten to 12m, allow 4-5 touches. Make it harder: only 2 touches over 18m (bigger pushes, faster sprint).

## Driving Technique — 15 min

Build the driving mechanic with progressive complexity: straight line → acceleration burst → direction change → end product. Every rep ends with a purpose (finish or pass to target).

**1. Push-and-chase — 4 min**
- 2 cones 20m apart, mini-goal behind the end cone
- Felix starts at cone A. Pushes ball hard with outside of foot (3-4m ahead), sprints to it, pushes again, repeats until end cone. Finishes into mini-goal.
- 8 reps: 4 right foot, 4 left foot
- Rest: walk-back recovery between reps (~20 sec)

```
              20m
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━[GOAL]
F ──●━━→  ●━━→  ●━━→  ●━━→ ⚽
   push  sprint push  sprint FINISH
```

**Coaching points:**
- Touch must be firm enough that he has to genuinely SPRINT — if he jogs to the ball, the push was too soft
- Contact point: laces or outside of foot, ball stays on the ground
- Last touch before the goal should be a controlled finish — drive hard, finish clean

> Make it easier: 15m distance, 3 pushes. Make it harder: coach shouts "LEFT" or "RIGHT" mid-run — Felix must push ball to that side and adjust angle to still finish.

**2. Acceleration gate drives — 5 min**  
- 25m lane. Cone A (start), gate of 2 cones (1m apart) at 8m, second gate at 16m, mini-goal at 25m
- Felix drives from A → cruises to gate 1 → EXPLODES through gate 1 → drives at speed → EXPLODES through gate 2 → finishes
- 8 reps alternating feet
- Rest: walk-back recovery

```
              25m
○━━━━━━━╫━━━━━━━━╫━━━━━━━━━[GOAL]
F ──●→ GATE1  → GATE2  →→→  ⚽
       (8m)     (16m)      FINISH
       ↑ explode  ↑ explode
```

**Coaching points:**
- The gates represent moments in a game where space opens up — the accelerations are the moment he DECIDES to drive
- Body drops slightly lower through the gate — like a sprinter out of the blocks
- "This is the moment in a game when a defender steps off you and there's 5m of grass — you GO"

> Make it easier: one gate only at 10m. Make it harder: add a sharp cut (90°) after gate 2 before finishing — drive, cut, drive, finish.

**3. Drive and cut to finish — 6 min**
- L-shaped setup: cone A (start), cone B (12m ahead), mini-goal 8m to the right of B
- Felix drives A → B at speed, performs a sharp cut at B (inside or outside of foot), then immediately drives toward the mini-goal and finishes
- 8 reps: 4 cutting right, 4 cutting left (mirror the setup)
- Rest: walk-back recovery

```
                   [GOAL]
                     ↑
                     ↑  8m drive
    12m              ↑
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
A                   B ──→ cut + drive to goal
F ──●━━→  →  →  →  ↗
    drive at speed
```

**Coaching points:**
- The cut is ONE sharp touch — sole, inside, or outside — then IMMEDIATELY push the ball ahead and drive again. No stuttering, no extra touches
- Before the cut: head up to "see" the goal position — this simulates scanning in the game
- "In a game this is: you receive centrally, drive forward, a defender slides across, you cut and drive into the space he left"

> Make it easier: slow the approach, cut at 60%. Make it harder: coach stands near B as a passive body — Felix must cut AWAY from the coach and finish.

## Drive or Pass? Decision-Making — 17 min

Felix's elite passing is now an asset, not a crutch. We create scenarios where the environment forces him to READ the situation and CHOOSE: drive or pass. CLA principle — the constraint teaches, the coach stays quiet.

**1. Gate read: drive through or pass aside — 6 min**
- 20x15m channel. Felix starts with ball at the bottom centre
- 2 cones form a gate in the centre of the channel at 10m
- Gate is either OPEN (cones 4m apart, inviting the drive) or CLOSED (cones 1m apart, blocking the drive)
- Mini-goal at the far end (20m), second mini-goal offset 8m to the left
- Coach sets the gate width BEFORE each rep (Felix doesn't see until he looks up with the ball)
- **Open gate → Felix drives through and finishes in the far goal**
- **Closed gate → Felix plays a pass to the side goal and follows the pass**
- 12 reps — random gate settings

```
  OPEN scenario:                  CLOSED scenario:

○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○            ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
┃     [GOAL far]     ┃            ┃     [GOAL far]     ┃
┃         ↑          ┃            ┃                    ┃
┃    ○    ↑    ○     ┃  4m gap    ┃       ○○           ┃  1m gap
┃  GATE──↑──GATE     ┃            ┃    GATE GATE       ┃
┃         ↑          ┃            ┃     ╲              ┃
┃         ↑          ┃   15m      ┃      ╲→ [GOAL     ┃   15m
┃                    ┃            ┃         side]      ┃
┃     F ──●          ┃            ┃     F ──●──→       ┃
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○            ○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
       20m                              20m

  → DRIVE through, finish          → PASS to side goal
```

**Coaching points:**
- Felix must look up EARLY — read the gate width with his first glance, not after receiving
- Open gate = "there's space between the lines in a real game — go!" Closed gate = "the central lane is blocked — switch it, play the pass"
- The quality of the decision matters more than the quality of the finish — praise correct reads
- "In a game, this gate is the gap between two defenders. Sometimes it's open. Sometimes it's not. You need to SEE it before you get there."

> Make it easier: gate always starts clearly open or clearly closed. Make it harder: gate starts at medium width (2m) — Felix must judge whether it's driveable or not. This is where the real game brain develops.

**2. Live read: coach as defender — 5 min**
- Same 20x15m channel. Mini-goal at 20m, second mini-goal offset at 8m left
- Remove the gate cones. Coach now stands at the 10m line as a LIVE semi-active defender
- Coach either:
  - **Steps forward toward Felix** (pressing) → Felix should recognise pressure and PASS to side goal
  - **Holds position or backpedals** (deep) → Felix should recognise space and DRIVE past to far goal
- Felix starts with ball from bottom. Coach varies behaviour randomly.
- 10 reps — coach increases activity level as Felix reads better
- No verbal signal from coach — Felix reads BODY LANGUAGE only

```
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
┃      [GOAL far]       ┃
┃                       ┃
┃                       ┃
┃     K (coach/def)     ┃  ← 10m line. Steps up OR holds
┃     ↑    or    ↗      ┃
┃     ↑         ↗       ┃      20m
┃     ↑        ↗        ┃
┃  [GOAL      F ──●     ┃
┃  side]                ┃
○━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━○
          15m
```

**Coaching points:**
- No coaching during reps — let Felix solve it. Only intervene AFTER a set of 3-4 reps: "What made you decide to drive/pass?"
- If Felix always passes (his comfort zone), say nothing — he'll eventually see the open lane and try driving. The constraints teach.
- "Read the defender's hips and feet — if they're coming at you, they can't cover the pass. If they're sitting, you can drive."

> Make it easier: coach exaggerates movements (big press or clearly sits deep). Make it harder: coach disguises — starts pressing then stops, or holds then steps — Felix must react to what's happening in the MOMENT.

**3. Receive-and-decide: 3-target game — 6 min**
- Diamond shape: coach at top (A), Felix at bottom (B), mini-goal to the left at C (8m from B), mini-goal to the right at D (8m from B), cone-goal straight ahead at E (15m from B, past A)
- Coach plays ball to Felix at B
- Before the ball arrives: coach drops a cone as a "defender" in one of three positions:
  - Cone dropped CENTRALLY between B and E → path ahead blocked → Felix passes to C or D (nearest open goal)
  - Cone dropped NEAR C → left lane blocked → Felix drives to E (straight) or passes to D
  - Cone dropped NEAR D → right lane blocked → Felix drives to E or passes to C
- Felix scans WHILE the ball is travelling — sees where the cone lands — decides
- 12 reps — random cone drops

```
              E [GOAL straight]
              ↑   15m from B
              ↑
         A (coach)
        ╱    ↑    ╲
       ╱     ↑     ╲
      ╱    pass     ╲
[GOAL]                [GOAL]
  C  ←─── B (Felix) ───→ D
         ──●
     8m left      8m right
```

**Coaching points:**
- The scan must happen WHILE the ball is in the air — not after receiving. "The ball is your clock — by the time it arrives, you already know"
- This simulates a central player's real decision matrix: play left, play right, or drive forward
- Encourage Felix to take his touch in the DIRECTION of his decision — first touch = decision. No receiving and THEN looking up

> Make it easier: coach verbally says "left blocked" instead of dropping a cone. Make it harder: two cones dropped — two lanes blocked — Felix has only one option and must find it instantly.

## Movement to Receive & Drive — 12 min

Connect everything: how Felix GETS free to receive the ball in a position where he can drive. A central player who can drive is worthless if he can't create the space to receive facing forward.

**1. Check away, check to — receive and drive to goal — 6 min**
- Setup: cone A (coach with ball), cone B (12m from A, Felix's starting position), cone C (3m behind B, fake-run target), mini-goal 10m ahead of B
- Sequence:
  1. Felix jogs to cone C (checking AWAY — selling a run to take the imaginary marker with him)
  2. Sharp change of direction — SNAPS back toward coach at A
  3. Coach plays ball into Felix's front foot
  4. Felix takes a DRIVING first touch forward (not a control touch) and drives 10m to the mini-goal, finishes
- 10 reps: 5 receiving left side, 5 right side (mirror the setup)
- Rest: walk-back recovery

```
                   [GOAL]
                     ↑
                     ↑  10m drive + finish
                     ↑
            ② snap back
    C ←←←←← B ─────→ drive ──→ ⚽
     ① fake    ↑
     run away  ↑ pass
     (3m)      ↑
               A (coach)
              12m
```

**Coaching points:**
- The fake run (check away) must be SHARP and committed — 3 quick steps away, then EXPLOSIVE change of direction back. "If the fake looks lazy, the marker doesn't buy it"
- First touch direction = forward and into space. NOT a cushion stop. "Your first touch IS the drive"
- Shoulder check happens during the fake run — Felix looks over his shoulder to see the space he's about to drive into BEFORE he receives
- "This is what Pedri does 30 times a game — check away, snap back, receive, drive. The defender follows the fake and you're gone"

> Make it easier: no finish, just drive to end cone. Make it harder: coach adds a passive defender at B who tries to follow the fake run — Felix must time the snap-back to lose the defender.

**2. Half-turn receive and drive — 6 min**
- Setup: cone B (Felix, standing SIDEWAYS), coach at A (8m behind), mini-goal 12m ahead of B, cone "defender" (D) placed 3m behind/beside Felix
- Coach plays ball firmly to Felix's back foot
- Felix receives on the half-turn (inside of foot, body opens), takes the touch past the defender cone, and drives 12m to the mini-goal to finish
- 10 reps: 5 opening to the left, 5 opening to the right
- Progress after 6 reps: move the defender cone to different angles — Felix must adjust which way he opens

```
                    [GOAL]
                      ↑
                      ↑  12m drive + finish
                      ↑
              D ── B (Felix, sideways)
        defender     ╱↑
         cone       ╱ ↑
          (3m)     ╱  ↑  receives on half-turn,
                  ╱   ↑  opens body, drives forward
                 A (coach)
                 8m behind
```

**Coaching points:**
- Body position BEFORE the ball arrives: hips half-open, weight on front foot, ready to turn. "If your body is square to the passer, you can't turn — set yourself up before the ball comes"
- The receiving touch and the first driving touch are ONE movement — inside of foot opens the body, ball rolls forward, feet are already moving. No stop.
- "This is how you play between the lines. The ball comes from behind you, a defender is on your shoulder — you need to know which side to turn to and GO"
- Head checks over the shoulder 1-2 times before the ball arrives — know where the defender cone is, turn AWAY from it

> Make it easier: no defender cone, just turn and drive. Make it harder: coach varies the pass — sometimes to feet, sometimes slightly off to one side — Felix must adjust his turn.

## Cool Down & Reflection — 5 min

- Light jog with ball — soft, playful touches, both feet, alternating sole rolls and inside taps — 2 min
- Static stretching: quads, hamstrings, hip flexors, calves — 2 min
- **Reflection questions (guided discovery — ask, don't tell):**
  - "When did it feel RIGHT to drive today? What was different about those moments compared to when you passed?"
  - "What do you look at to decide — drive or pass? What are you scanning for?"
  - "When you received the ball and drove forward, what did your first touch feel like compared to when you receive and pass?"
  - "In the tournament, when you get the ball centrally and there's space — what are you going to do?"

> **Connection to next session:** Build on today's driving by introducing 1v1 situations where Felix must drive PAST a live defender — not just into space. Add more movement patterns to get free as a central player (false 9 drops, midfielder rotations). Begin combining: drive → draw defender → release the pass. The full central player toolkit.
