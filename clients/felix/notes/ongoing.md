## 2026-04-15 — Team training observation

Team session theme was receiving on the half-turn. Felix's technique on the half-turn was actually decent — body shape was better than most. But the same pattern from the individual assessment showed up clearly: even when he received on the half-turn and had space to drive into, he chose the safe pass back. The "drive or pass?" decision-making we worked on yesterday hasn't transferred yet. In the directional rondo he played every ball short and sideways — never once drove through the middle when the defender was beaten.

Key takeaways:
- Body shape and receiving technique is ahead of peers — the individual session on driving touched the right area
- The core issue confirmed: it's not physical ability to drive, it's the decision/confidence to DO it in a game context with real pressure
- Yesterday's session was day 1 on driving — needs many more reps before it becomes a genuine option in his decision tree
- Next individual session: more constrained games where driving is rewarded (e.g. bonus points for driving past a line) — environment must demand it, not just allow it
- Movement off the ball was passive — stood still waiting for the ball instead of checking away from markers and creating an angle

## 2026-04-14 — Initial assessment

Felix is technically very clean. Great passing foot and always picks the right pass. The key development area is dynamism — he defaults to the safe pass even when driving forward is the better option. He needs to develop the physical ability to carry the ball at speed AND the decision-making to recognise when to drive vs. pass. Secondary focus is his movement off the ball — how to get free of markers as a central player, checking shoulders, creating angles. Intensity is always threaded into every session.
