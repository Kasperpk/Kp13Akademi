## Sprint Priming & Warm-Up — 8 min

Get the body ready for explosive work before any sprinting. Do each in a line (~10m) and back.

- **High knees** — Jump knees (runners warmup)
- **Heel kicks** — flick heels up to glutes, stay on toes, 2x down-and-back
- **Single leg hops** — 5 hops on right, 5 on left, focus on soft landing
- **Broad jumps** — 5 two-footed jumps forward, stick the landing each time
- **Move goal** - 5 times pushing a goal
- **Short ball taps** — 30 seconds toe taps on ball, light and quick

> Coaching cue: arms drive the sprint — make sure Sofus pumps his arms properly from the start.

---

## Sprint & Explosiveness — 15 min

Focus: first 3-5 steps acceleration. Sofus is physically strong — we want that strength to translate into explosive first movement.

**1. Reaction start sprints**
- 8 times stay on toes - sprint on call (lean abit forward, use arms, bigger and bigger steps)

**2. Two-step forward, two-step back**
- 8 Two-steps forward two steps back sprint forward, then backwards

**3. Turn and sprint**
- Weaving sprints

> Coaching cue: "low and drive" in the first 3 steps — chest down, not upright.

## Passing & Receiving — 15 min

Central midfielder-specific. Receive with purpose, play quickly, find the next action.

**1. Two-touch wall passes**
- 3m from wall
- Pass, let it return, control with 1 touch, pass back with 2nd touch
- 20 reps right foot, 20 reps left foot
- Builds quick feet and clean first touch under low pressure

**2. Receive and play forward (with coach)**
- Coach stands 8m away
- Plays ball to Sofus's feet
- Sofus takes touch to the side (out of pressure) and plays back to a cone/target 5m to the side
- Simulates: receive from CB, play to winger
- 10 reps left side, 10 reps right side

**3. Turn and find the pass**
- Coach plays ball to Sofus's back foot
- Sofus takes a receiving touch to turn (inside or outside)
- Immediately plays a pass to a target cone
- 10 reps, mix of inside and outside turns
- Focus: the turn and the pass should feel like one movement

> Coaching cue: before the ball arrives, look up — Sofus should already know where the pass is going before he touches it.
