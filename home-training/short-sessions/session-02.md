# Home Session 02 — Turns

**Time:** 12-15 minutes  
**Equipment:** 1 ball, 2 cones or water bottles  
**Space:** 5-6 steps of open space  

Turning with the ball lets you change direction quickly without losing control. Good turns help you escape defenders and find space.

---

## 1. Warm-Up — 2 minutes

Dribble slowly back and forth in your space. Keep the ball close, use both feet.

---

## 2. Inside Cut — 4 minutes

Dribble toward a cone, then cut the ball sharply with the inside of your foot and go the other way.

**How to do it:**
- Place 2 cones about 5 steps apart
- Dribble to one cone, cut with right foot inside, go back to the other cone
- 8 turns on right foot, then 8 turns on left foot

**Tip:** Plant your standing foot next to the ball before you cut. The cut comes from your ankle, not your whole leg.

**Easier:** Do the cut slowly — no need to sprint between cones.  
**Harder:** Add a small sprint after the cut.

---

## 3. Drag-Back — 4 minutes

Roll the ball backwards with the sole of your foot, then push it forward in a new direction.

**How to do it:**
- Dribble slowly forward
- Stop: put your foot on top of the ball and drag it back toward you
- Push the ball forward with the inside of the same foot
- Repeat 10 times right foot, 10 times left foot

**Tip:** Drag back, then push forward at a slight angle — not straight back the way you came.

**Easier:** Stand still, just practice the drag-back and push without dribbling first.  
**Harder:** Do it faster, or add a direction change after the push.

---

## 4. Cool Down — 1-2 minutes

Touch the ball softly between feet while walking slowly. Focus on the feel of the ball.

---

## Well done! Try to do this 2-3 times this week.
