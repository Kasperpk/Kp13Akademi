# Home Session 04 — Ball Familiarity & Coordination

**Time:** 12-15 minutes  
**Equipment:** 1 ball  
**Space:** Just enough to stand — you barely need to move  

This session is about getting comfortable with the ball. The best players in the world feel like the ball is part of their foot. That feeling comes from thousands of small touches — exactly like these.

---

## 1. Toe Taps — 3 minutes

Alternate tapping the top of the ball with your right and left foot toes. Keep the ball still.

**How to do it:**
- Tap right foot, tap left foot, right, left — keep going for 30 seconds
- Rest 15 seconds
- Repeat 3 times

**Tip:** Stay on the balls of your feet (not flat-footed). Keep your body upright.

**Easier:** Go slowly — no rush.  
**Harder:** Speed up each set.

---

## 2. Inside-Outside Rolls — 4 minutes

Roll the ball side to side under your foot — inside of the foot, then outside.

**How to do it:**
- Right foot: push ball left with inside, pull back right with outside — 20 times
- Left foot: same — 20 times
- Alternate feet: 20 times each side

**Tip:** Keep the movement controlled. The ball shouldn't roll too far — small, clean movements.

**Easier:** Do it with one foot only until it feels comfortable.  
**Harder:** Add a small side-step with your body as you roll.

---

## 3. Sole Rolls + Quick Tap — 4 minutes

Sole-roll the ball to the side, then quickly tap it back with the inside of the same foot.

**How to do it:**
- Right foot: roll right with sole, tap back left with inside — 15 times
- Left foot: same — 15 times
- Alternate: 10 times each

**Tip:** The tap back should be quick — don't let the ball get away from you.

---

## 4. Cool Down — 1-2 minutes

Juggle the ball if you can (or try to!). Even 1-2 touches before it drops is great. No pressure — just play.

---

## Well done! The more you touch the ball, the more natural it feels.
