# Home Session 03 — Weak Foot

**Time:** 12-15 minutes  
**Equipment:** 1 ball, a wall  
**Space:** Any small space  

Most players have a stronger foot and a weaker foot. Practicing your weaker foot makes you twice as dangerous — defenders don't know which way you'll go. This session is all left foot (if your right is stronger) or all right foot (if your left is stronger).

---

## 1. Warm-Up — 2 minutes

Tap the ball with only your weak foot — back and forth, side to side. Just get comfortable.

---

## 2. Weak Foot Wall Passes — 5 minutes

Kick the ball against the wall with your weak foot only. Stop it with your weak foot when it comes back.

**How to do it:**
- Stand 2-3 steps from the wall
- Kick and stop — 15 times, slow pace
- Kick and stop — 15 times, slightly faster
- Kick and immediately kick again without stopping — 10 times

**Tip:** It will feel clumsy at first. That's normal. Don't give up — this is exactly what practice is for.

**Easier:** Stand closer to the wall.  
**Harder:** Try hitting a specific spot on the wall each time.

---

## 3. Weak Foot Dribbling — 5 minutes

Dribble using only your weak foot. Use the inside, outside, and sole.

**How to do it:**
- Dribble in a straight line and back — 4 times (inside of foot only)
- Dribble in a straight line and back — 4 times (outside of foot only)
- Dribble in a figure-8 pattern around 2 objects — 4 times

**Tip:** Short touches. Don't kick the ball too far ahead — keep it close to your foot.

**Easier:** Go as slow as you need.  
**Harder:** Speed up on the straight lines.

---

## 4. Cool Down — 1-2 minutes

Tap the ball with both feet alternately — right, left, right, left — 30 taps total.

---

## Well done! Your weak foot gets stronger every time you practice it.
