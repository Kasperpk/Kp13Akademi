# Home Session 01 — First Touch

**Time:** 12-15 minutes  
**Equipment:** 1 ball, a wall or flat surface  
**Space:** Any small space — garden, garage, hallway  

Practice controlling the ball cleanly the first time you touch it. A good first touch gives you more time and makes everything easier.

---

## 1. Warm-Up — 2 minutes

Walk around slowly and tap the ball back and forth between your feet. Low touches, keep the ball close.

---

## 2. Wall Passes — 5 minutes

Kick the ball gently against a wall and stop it with the inside of your foot when it comes back.

**How to do it:**
- Stand 2-3 steps from the wall
- Kick with your right foot, stop with your right foot — 10 times
- Kick with your left foot, stop with your left foot — 10 times
- Alternate feet — 20 times

**Tip:** The moment the ball touches your foot, pull your foot back slightly — this softens the ball and keeps it close.

**Easier:** Stand closer to the wall so the ball comes back slower.  
**Harder:** Try to stop the ball and immediately pass it back without pausing.

---

## 3. Sole Control — 5 minutes

Place your foot on top of the ball (the sole) and roll it side to side.

**How to do it:**
- Right foot: roll the ball left and right 20 times
- Left foot: roll the ball left and right 20 times
- Alternate feet: 30 rolls total

Then try moving forward slowly while rolling the ball under your sole.

**Tip:** Stay on your toes — don't flat-foot it.

**Easier:** Do it standing still before moving.  
**Harder:** Speed up or try going backwards.

---

## 4. Cool Down — 1-2 minutes

Juggle the ball or just tap it softly between feet while standing still. No pressure — just touch the ball gently.

---

## Well done! Try to do this 2-3 times this week.
